## Outline

### Introduction (~180 words)

- Hook: a Bali villa is both a property and a hospitality business; due diligence has to test both.
- Problem: photos, projected ROI, and seller-provided documents are not enough.
- Promise: a practical checklist for direct buyers, passive investors, sellers, and developers.
- Compliance note: educational content only, not legal, tax, or financial advice.

### H2: What Is Property Due Diligence In Bali? (~250 words)

- Answer-first opener: due diligence verifies whether the property can legally, physically, and financially do what the seller or platform says it can do.
- Cover legal, physical, operational, financial, and fractional/platform due diligence.
- Explain why Bali property checks are different for foreigners.
- Internal link zone: `bali-property-investment-guide-2026` if present in production; otherwise `real-estate-101`.
- Visual: simple five-part checklist table.

### H2: Which Ownership Or Investment Structure Are You Using? (~320 words)

- Answer-first opener: the right checklist depends on whether you are using leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA/HGB, or fractional exposure.
- Cover:
  - Leasehold as a contract-based right.
  - Hak Pakai as a registered right for eligible foreigners.
  - PT PMA/HGB as a company route with compliance burden.
  - Fractional/economic exposure through a platform.
  - Nominee arrangements as a major red flag.
- Chart suggestion: comparison table by structure, document to review, and core risk.
- Source requirements: UU No. 5/1960, PP No. 18/2021, Indonesian counsel review.

### H2: How Do You Verify Title, Seller Authority, And Encumbrances? (~330 words)

- Answer-first opener: title and authority checks should be independent, not seller-led.
- Key points:
  - Confirm original certificate details.
  - Verify through qualified PPAT/notary and BPN process.
  - Match seller identity or corporate authority to the transaction.
  - Check marital/spousal consent where applicable.
  - Check liens, disputes, tax arrears, blocks, inheritance claims, and boundary issues.
- Red flags:
  - Pressure to deposit before verification.
  - Seller refuses independent review.
  - Only seller's lawyer/notary is involved.
- Source requirements: BPN/PPAT process references, legal review.

### H2: How Do You Check Zoning, Building Permits, And Rental Legality? (~330 words)

- Answer-first opener: a villa can be attractive but still fail as an investment if its use is not permitted.
- Key points:
  - Confirm zoning and intended use.
  - Verify PBG or legacy IMB status.
  - Verify SLF/functionality status where applicable.
  - Confirm short-term rental and hospitality licensing path.
  - Check whether built area, number of floors, and use match approved documents.
- Chart suggestion: document stack from land title to operating license.
- Compliance risk: rules can vary by locality; avoid definitive legal advice.

### H2: What Physical And Technical Checks Should You Run? (~280 words)

- Answer-first opener: physical due diligence tests whether the property can operate safely and predictably.
- Key points:
  - Legal road access.
  - Water source, drainage, electricity capacity, internet.
  - Flooding, slope, soil stability, erosion, retaining walls.
  - Roof, waterproofing, plumbing, electrical, humidity, structural condition.
  - Neighboring plots and construction risk.
- Image placement: site inspection, access road, or construction detail.

### H2: How Do You Review The Lease Or Purchase Contract? (~280 words)

- Answer-first opener: headline terms are not enough; extension, transfer, use, and dispute clauses determine the real risk.
- Key points:
  - Term length and renewal/extension mechanics.
  - Renewal pricing formula.
  - Transfer, sale, sublease, and rental rights.
  - Renovation, rebuilding, and insurance obligations.
  - Early termination, breach, force majeure, governing law, dispute forum.
- Pull quote idea: "A 25+25 lease is only as strong as the extension clause."

### H2: How Do You Test The ROI Assumptions? (~330 words)

- Answer-first opener: projected gross rental revenue is not the same as net investor return.
- Key points:
  - Occupancy by season.
  - ADR assumptions and comparables.
  - Management fees.
  - Maintenance reserve.
  - Insurance.
  - Taxes/licensing costs.
  - Platform fees where relevant.
  - Currency risk.
  - Exit/liquidity assumptions.
- Chart suggestion: waterfall from gross rent to net investor distribution.
- Source requirements: BPS Bali tourism data only for demand context; do not use it as property-level ROI proof.

### H2: What Should Fractional Investors Check Before Investing Through POOOL? (~340 words)

- Answer-first opener: fractional investors do not need to personally run every title check, but they should receive clear evidence that the platform reviewed the right risks.
- Checklist:
  - Property review summary.
  - SPV or holding-structure explanation.
  - Economic-rights/investor agreement.
  - Escrow/payment flow.
  - Operator/manager identity and role.
  - Fee schedule.
  - Distribution policy and timing.
  - Risk disclosures.
  - Exit/liquidity limits.
  - Reporting cadence.
- CTA: Review the project documents before investing.
- Compliance risk: confirm every POOOL-specific claim with legal/product before drafting.

### H2: What Are The Biggest Bali Property Red Flags? (~260 words)

- Answer-first opener: the biggest red flags are usually unclear rights, missing approvals, rushed payments, and return claims that hide assumptions.
- List:
  - Guaranteed ROI or risk-free language.
  - Nominee ownership claims.
  - No independent BPN/PPAT/lawyer check.
  - Unclear zoning or rental legality.
  - Missing PBG/IMB or SLF where needed.
  - Vague lease-extension terms.
  - No legal road access.
  - ROI model excludes tax, fees, maintenance, seasonality, or currency.
  - Platform cannot explain SPV, documents, operator role, or exit limits.
- Visual: severity/action risk matrix.

## FAQ Section (~450 words)

1. Can foreigners own freehold property in Bali?
   - Direction: explain that foreign individuals generally cannot personally hold Hak Milik/freehold land; legal routes depend on structure and eligibility.
2. Is leasehold property safe in Bali?
   - Direction: it can be workable when properly documented, but terms, authority, permitted use, and counterparty risk matter.
3. What is the most important due diligence check?
   - Direction: independent verification of title, authority, encumbrances, zoning, permitted use, and contract terms before major payments.
4. Do I need a lawyer or notary?
   - Direction: direct buyers/lessees should use qualified independent advisers; fractional investors should still review platform documents and disclosures.
5. Can due diligence guarantee returns?
   - Direction: no. It reduces avoidable risk but cannot guarantee occupancy, resale value, exchange rates, income, or liquidity.
