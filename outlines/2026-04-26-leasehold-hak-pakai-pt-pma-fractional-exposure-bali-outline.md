### Introduction: Why Bali Property "Ownership" Needs A Clearer Definition (~180 words)

- Hook: most Bali property confusion starts when one word, ownership, is used for several different rights.
- Introduce the four routes: leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA/HGB, fractional exposure.
- State this is educational, not legal, tax, or financial advice.
- Promise comparison by eligibility, rights, control, cost, duration, liquidity, and risk.
- Place a short "Key Takeaways" box after the introduction.

### H2: What Can Foreigners Actually Own Or Control In Bali? (~350 words)

- Answer-first opener: foreign individuals generally cannot personally hold Hak Milik/freehold land, but may encounter use rights, company-held rights, lease contracts, or economic exposure.
- Key points:
  - Difference between Hak Milik/freehold, Hak Pakai, HGB, lease contracts, and economic rights.
  - Why registered rights, contract rights, and platform/investor rights are not interchangeable.
  - Why marketing language should be read against the documents.

### H2: How Does Leasehold Work In Bali? (~320 words)

- Answer-first opener: leasehold is a contract-based right to use land or a property for an agreed period, not personal freehold ownership.
- Key points:
  - Typical buyer/investor use case.
  - Lease term and extension mechanism.
  - Transfer, sublease, renovation, and rental-operation permissions.
  - Counterparty risk and remaining-term risk.
- H3: What to check before relying on "extendable"
  - Notice period, price formula, who must approve, what happens on dispute.
- Key statistic/source to find: official law for leasehold concept plus counsel review.
- Chart suggestion: Comparison table row.
- Image placement: No.

### H2: How Does Hak Pakai Work For Eligible Foreigners? (~320 words)

- Answer-first opener: Hak Pakai is a registered right to use property for eligible holders, but it is not the same as Hak Milik/freehold and may not fit every passive investor.
- Key points:
  - Eligibility and domicile/residency caveats.
  - Registered title distinction.
  - Residential/personal-use framing.
  - Renewal/extension and transfer caveats.
- H3: When Hak Pakai may not match the investor's goal
  - Passive income, small-ticket exposure, commercial operation, and liquidity needs.
- Key statistic/source to find: PP No. 18/2021 provisions and Indonesian counsel review.
- Chart suggestion: Comparison table row.
- Image placement: No.

### H2: How Does PT PMA With HGB Work? (~350 words)

- Answer-first opener: a PT PMA/HGB route can support company-based commercial property activity, but it brings setup, licensing, tax, reporting, and governance complexity.
- Key points:
  - PT PMA basics for foreign investment.
  - HGB/right-to-build concept in simple terms.
  - OSS/risk-based licensing, NIB, tax, and reporting expectations.
  - Why company control can be useful but not automatically simpler or better.
- H3: What compliance obligations can change the economics?
  - Setup cost, accounting, tax, local licensing, reporting, and operator burden.
- Key statistic/source to find: PP No. 18/2021, GR 28/2025 licensing references, counsel/accounting review.

  - SPV/holding/entity explanation only if approved by POOOL legal/product.

### FAQ Section (~450 words)

1. Can foreigners own freehold land in Bali?
   - Direction: generally no personal Hak Milik/freehold ownership; cite official law and recommend counsel.
2. Is leasehold the same as ownership?
   - Direction: no, it is a contractual use right under agreed terms.
3. Is Hak Pakai better than leasehold?
   - Direction: depends on eligibility, use case, residency, and risk tolerance.
4. Do I need a PT PMA to invest in Bali property?
   - Direction: not always; direct commercial operation differs from passive economic exposure.
5. Does fractional exposure mean I own the land?
   - Direction: rights depend on documents; avoid direct land-ownership claims unless approved.
