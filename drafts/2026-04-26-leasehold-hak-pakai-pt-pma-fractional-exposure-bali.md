# Leasehold vs Hak Pakai vs PT PMA vs Fractional Exposure in Bali

Most Bali property confusion starts with one word: ownership.

A leasehold villa, a Hak Pakai title, a PT PMA structure, and a fractional investment can all give someone exposure to Bali property. They do not give the same rights, control, duties, or exit options. Treating them as interchangeable is where legal, tax, and investment risk starts.

This guide compares the four routes a foreign buyer or investor is most likely to hear about: leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA/HGB, and fractional exposure through a structured platform such as POOOL.

This is educational content, not legal, tax, or financial advice. Indonesian property-law claims should be checked by qualified Indonesian counsel before any transaction or publication.

> **Key Takeaways**
> - Foreign individuals should be cautious with personal freehold claims in Indonesia. The official Basic Agrarian Law and later land-rights rules distinguish between Hak Milik, Hak Pakai, HGB, lease rights, and other rights.
> - Leasehold is contract-led. Hak Pakai is a registered right for eligible users. PT PMA/HGB is a company route with setup and compliance duties. Fractional exposure is document-led economic participation.
> - Fractional investors should focus less on personal land title and more on the legal documents, asset entity, fee schedule, distribution policy, operator role, risk disclosure, and exit limits.
> - No route removes risk. The right question is not "Which one is safest?" It is "Which risks am I taking, who controls them, and where are they documented?"

## What Can Foreigners Actually Own or Control in Bali?

Foreign investors need to separate land ownership from property exposure.

Indonesia's land-rights system is not a simple freehold-vs-leasehold model. Law No. 5 of 1960 on Basic Agrarian Principles lists several land rights, including Hak Milik, Hak Guna Bangunan, Hak Pakai, and lease-related rights. Later rules, including Government Regulation No. 18 of 2021, update the framework for land rights, apartment units, and registration.

For a foreign reader, the practical point is this: a structure may give use, control, company-held rights, or economic exposure without giving personal Hak Milik freehold land ownership.

That distinction matters because marketing language often compresses different rights into the same phrase. "Own a Bali villa" can mean:

- You lease the property for a defined term.
- You hold or benefit from a registered use right, if eligible.
- Your Indonesian foreign-investment company holds relevant rights.
- You participate economically through platform documents.
- Someone else claims to hold freehold title for you, which needs careful legal review and may be a red flag.

POOOL note: for passive investors, the core question is usually not "Can I personally hold the title?" It is "What legal and economic rights do my documents give me, and what risks remain?"

## Quick Comparison: Leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA/HGB, and Fractional Exposure

| Route | Main idea | Typical fit | Investor control | Key review point |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leasehold | Contractual right to use property for a defined term | Personal use, rental operation, smaller direct buyer route | Medium, based on contract terms | Lease term, extension, transfer, rental permission, seller authority |
| Hak Pakai | Registered right to use land/property, subject to eligibility and rules | Eligible foreign resident or personal-use scenario | Medium, subject to title and conditions | Eligibility, title registration, permitted use, term, transfer limits |
| PT PMA/HGB | Company route where a foreign-investment company may hold relevant rights | Commercial operator, developer, larger direct acquisition | Higher operational control, higher compliance burden | Corporate setup, licensing, tax, title, governance, reporting |
| Fractional exposure | Economic participation through platform/legal documents | Passive investor seeking smaller-ticket exposure | Lower direct control, document-defined economics | Investor agreement, asset entity, fees, distributions, reporting, exit limits |

This table is a starting point, not a recommendation. The right structure depends on residency, use case, budget, intended rental activity, need for control, time horizon, tax position, and legal eligibility.

## How Does Leasehold Work in Bali?

Leasehold is a contract-based route. Instead of personally holding freehold land, the buyer or investor receives a contractual right to use a property for a defined period under agreed terms.

That can be useful when the goal is personal use, rental operation, or exposure to a specific villa without setting up a foreign-investment company. It can also be misunderstood. A lease is only as strong as its parties, documents, permitted use, extension language, and enforcement path.

Before relying on a leasehold structure, review:

- Who legally owns the land and has authority to lease it.
- Whether the lease is properly documented and, where relevant, notarized or registered.
- The remaining term and the exact start date.
- Extension rights and how the future extension price is calculated.
- Whether rental, sublease, renovation, rebuilding, transfer, or sale of lease rights is permitted.
- Who pays property taxes, community obligations, insurance, maintenance, and licensing costs.
- What happens after breach, early termination, disaster, sale of the underlying land, or death of a party.

The phrase "25 years plus extension" is not enough. The extension mechanism matters. If the extension depends on future consent, future pricing, or missing documentation, the investor has a different risk profile than the headline term suggests.

Leasehold can be workable when the documents are clear and verified. It should not be described as risk-free ownership.

## How Does Hak Pakai Work for Eligible Foreigners?

Hak Pakai is commonly translated as the right of use. It is a registered land right under Indonesia's land-rights framework, and official rules set eligibility and conditions for who may hold or benefit from it.

For foreign individuals, Hak Pakai is often discussed in personal residence or long-stay contexts. It can be more formal than a private lease because it is a registered right, but it is still not the same as personal Hak Milik freehold ownership.

The due diligence is different from leasehold. A buyer needs to check:

- Whether they are eligible for the structure at the time of acquisition and during ownership or use.
- Whether the property type, location, and use fit the rules.
- Whether the title is clean and properly registered.
- How long the right lasts and what renewal or extension process applies.
- Whether transfer, mortgage, inheritance, rental use, or change of use is allowed.
- What happens if residency status, eligibility, or use changes.

Hak Pakai may be relevant for some foreign buyers. It may not fit every remote investor, passive investor, rental operator, or small-ticket investment goal.

POOOL note: a passive fractional investor should not assume that Hak Pakai is the route behind every Bali property investment. The structure depends on the asset, entity, legal documents, and local counsel.

## How Does PT PMA With HGB Work?

PT PMA is a foreign-investment company. In property contexts, it is often discussed with Hak Guna Bangunan, or HGB, which is usually translated as the right to build. This route can make sense for larger commercial activity, development, hospitality operations, or investors who need a company to hold and operate assets.

The advantage is not simplicity. The advantage is structure and control when the business case supports the setup.

A PT PMA route may involve:

- Company incorporation and shareholder governance.
- Foreign investment rules and business classification checks.
- Minimum capital and reporting obligations.
- Tax registration, accounting, and ongoing compliance.
- Business licensing through the relevant Indonesian systems.
- Land-right verification and registration.
- Employment, management, insurance, construction, rental, and local operating requirements.

For a small passive investor, setting up a PT PMA can be too heavy for the goal. The cost and administrative burden may exceed the benefit. For an operator, developer, or larger buyer, the route may be worth reviewing with counsel and accountants.

The key is not to treat PT PMA as a magic workaround. It is a company with duties. Those duties can be significant.

## How Is Fractional Exposure Different From Owning or Leasing Directly?

Fractional exposure is a different category. It is usually not about you personally owning land or signing a direct villa lease. It is about participating economically through a platform and the legal documents attached to a specific asset or entity.

For POOOL-style fractional property investment, the investor should review what the documents actually say about:

- The asset or project being funded.
- The entity, SPV, holding structure, or contractual structure involved.
- The investor's economic rights.
- How rental revenue, expenses, reserves, fees, and distributions are calculated.
- Who controls property management and operator selection.
- How reports and updates are provided.
- What risk disclosures apply.
- Whether there is any exit route, transfer process, or secondary-market process, and what limits apply.

This route may reduce the need for each investor to negotiate Indonesian property documents alone. It does not remove legal, operating, currency, liquidity, market, or platform risk.

The compliance language matters. Unless approved legal documents support it, fractional investors should not be told they personally own Indonesian land. More careful phrasing is "fractional exposure," "economic participation," "contractual economic rights," or another counsel-approved term.

## Which Structure Fits Which Investor Scenario?

There is no universal winner. Each route solves a different problem.

| Investor scenario | Route often discussed | Why it may fit | What to verify first |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retiree or long-stay resident seeking personal use | Hak Pakai or leasehold | May align with personal residence needs | Eligibility, use rights, term, title, tax, succession |
| Remote investor seeking passive exposure | Fractional exposure | Smaller ticket size and no direct operation | Investor agreement, fee stack, distributions, operator, exit limits |
| Villa operator or hospitality business | PT PMA/HGB or leasehold | More control over operations | Licensing, corporate compliance, land rights, operating permits |
| First-time investor with limited capital | Fractional exposure | Access without buying a whole villa | Risk disclosure, platform role, reporting, liquidity assumptions |
| Buyer who wants maximum control | PT PMA/HGB or direct structure reviewed by counsel | Control may be higher if structure is appropriate | Setup cost, reporting, governance, tax, land status |
| Buyer who wants the simplest personal-use arrangement | Leasehold or Hak Pakai | May be simpler than a company if documents are clean | Contract terms, eligibility, transfer, extension, permitted use |

This is not personalized advice. It is a decision map. The final answer depends on facts that should be reviewed before money moves.

## What Are the Main Risks and Tradeoffs?

Every Bali property route has tradeoffs. The wrong structure can create risk even when the property itself looks attractive.

| Risk area | Leasehold | Hak Pakai | PT PMA/HGB | Fractional exposure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Legal enforceability | Depends on contract and authority | Depends on registration and eligibility | Depends on company and title compliance | Depends on platform and investor documents |
| Control | Medium | Medium | Higher | Lower direct control |
| Setup complexity | Low to medium | Medium | High | Lower for investor |
| Operating burden | Medium if rented | Medium if rented | High | Lower direct burden |
| Tax and reporting | Must be checked | Must be checked | Higher company burden | Platform and investor reporting need review |
| Liquidity | Buyer for remaining term needed | Transfer rules need review | Company/asset sale process may be complex | Exit may be limited or platform-defined |
| Return uncertainty | Property-level risk | Property-level risk | Property and business risk | Property, operator, platform, and liquidity risk |

Visual/chart idea: turn this table into a radar chart comparing control, complexity, liquidity, legal formality, and operating burden. Use cautious labels such as "higher" and "lower" instead of "safe" or "best."

The important point for investors is not that one route is always better. It is that each route moves risk to a different place.

- Leasehold risk often sits in the contract.
- Hak Pakai risk often sits in eligibility, title, and use conditions.
- PT PMA risk often sits in company compliance and operating obligations.
- Fractional exposure risk often sits in documents, platform governance, operator performance, and exit limits.

## What Documents Should You Review Before Choosing?

A serious comparison should end with documents, not slogans.

For a leasehold route, ask for:

- Land certificate and seller authority documents.
- Draft lease agreement.
- Extension and transfer clauses.
- Rental and sublease permissions.
- Renovation, rebuilding, and termination terms.
- Tax and cost allocation.
- Independent legal review notes.

For a Hak Pakai route, ask for:

- Title documentation.
- Eligibility analysis.
- Permitted-use review.
- Term, renewal, and transfer explanation.
- Tax and succession review.
- Independent notary or counsel confirmation.

For a PT PMA/HGB route, ask for:

- Company documents and shareholder structure.
- Business classification and licensing analysis.
- HGB or other land-right documents.
- NIB/OSS and relevant operating permits where applicable.
- Tax registration and reporting obligations.
- Governance, director authority, and signing authority.
- Accounting and annual compliance plan.

For fractional exposure, ask for:

- Project summary and investment memorandum.
- Asset or SPV structure explanation.
- Investor or economic-rights agreement.
- Fee schedule.
- Distribution policy.
- Risk disclosures.
- Property due-diligence summary.
- Operator or manager information.
- Reporting cadence.
- Exit, transfer, or liquidity terms.

POOOL note: fractional investing should make the document pack more readable, not less important. A lower ticket size does not remove the need to understand what you are buying.

Bali tourism data can help explain market context. It should not be used as proof of a specific villa's future return.

Those figures show that demand context matters. They do not prove that a specific villa will hit a projected occupancy rate, ADR, net yield, or resale value.

For investment review, tourism data should sit beside property-level evidence:

- Comparable nightly rates by area and season.
- Realistic occupancy assumptions.
- Operator track record.
- Cleaning, maintenance, insurance, tax, licensing, and reserve costs.
- Net distribution model after fees and reserves.
- Downside cases, not only base-case projections.

## FAQ

### Can foreigners own freehold land in Bali?

Foreigners should be cautious with personal freehold ownership claims. Indonesia's Basic Agrarian Law and related land-rights rules distinguish between several rights and eligible holders. Any claim involving Hak Milik, nominee ownership, Hak Pakai, HGB, leasehold, PT PMA, or fractional rights should be reviewed by Indonesian counsel.

### Is leasehold the same as ownership?

No. Leasehold is a contractual right to use a property under agreed terms. The lease may be valuable, transferable, or rentable if the documents allow it, but it should not be described as personal freehold land ownership.

### Is Hak Pakai better than leasehold?

Not automatically. Hak Pakai may offer registered-right formalities for eligible users, while leasehold may be simpler for some direct-use scenarios. The better route depends on eligibility, intended use, term, transfer needs, tax position, and legal review.

### Do I need a PT PMA to invest in Bali property?

Not always. PT PMA can be relevant for company-held property activity or commercial operations. A passive fractional investor may instead participate through platform documents. The key is to understand which rights, duties, costs, and risks the chosen route creates.

### Does fractional exposure mean I own the land?

Usually, fractional exposure should not be described as personal land ownership unless the legal documents and counsel approve that exact wording. Investors should read the agreement to understand whether they hold economic rights, contractual rights, shares, tokens, certificates, or another documented interest.

## Conclusion

Leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA/HGB, and fractional exposure are not four names for the same thing. They are four different ways to approach Bali property.

The responsible comparison is document-led:

- What right or exposure do you receive?
- Who controls the property?
- What costs and duties apply?
- What can go wrong?
- What happens if you want to exit?
- Which claims are verified by counsel, not marketing language?

For POOOL's audience, the most useful takeaway is simple: fractional exposure can make Bali property more accessible, but accessibility is not the same as certainty. Review the documents, understand the structure, and treat every projected return as an estimate.
