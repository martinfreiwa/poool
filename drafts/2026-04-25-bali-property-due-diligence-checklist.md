# Bali Property Due Diligence Checklist: What to Verify First

Most Bali property mistakes start before the contract is signed. A buyer falls in love with the photos. A developer leads with projected yield. A seller says the land title is clean. A rental manager says occupancy will take care of itself.

Due diligence is the discipline that slows that process down. It checks whether the property can legally, physically, and financially do what the seller or platform says it can do.

For fractional investors, the checklist is wider than a normal villa purchase. You are not only reviewing a property. You are reviewing the structure around it: the entity, economic rights, fees, disclosures, operator, escrow process, reporting, and exit limits.

This guide is educational, not legal, tax, or financial advice. Indonesian property law and licensing rules should be reviewed with qualified Indonesian counsel before money moves.

> **Key Takeaways**
> - Bali property due diligence should cover legal title, seller authority, zoning, building documentation, rental legality, access, utilities, physical condition, operating assumptions, and exit risk.
> - Foreign investors should be careful with personal freehold claims. Indonesia's Basic Agrarian Law and later land regulations distinguish between different land rights and eligible holders.
> - A villa is not just a building. For investment purposes, it is a hospitality micro-business with management, maintenance, taxes, occupancy, currency, and liquidity risk.
> - Fractional investors should review the platform's project documents, risk disclosure, economic-rights structure, fees, reporting cadence, and secondary-market limitations before investing.
> - Due diligence can reduce avoidable risk. It cannot guarantee occupancy, resale value, rental income, exchange rates, or future distributions.

## What Is Property Due Diligence in Bali?

Property due diligence is the process of verifying whether a Bali property is legally usable, physically sound, commercially realistic, and properly documented for the intended investment.

That means checking more than the visible villa. A good review covers five layers:

| Review layer | What it verifies | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Title, seller authority, encumbrances, lease rights, dispute history | Confirms whether the seller can legally transfer or grant rights |
| Planning and permits | Zoning, PBG or legacy IMB, SLF where applicable, rental permissions | Confirms whether the property can be built, occupied, and operated as planned |
| Physical and technical | Access, utilities, drainage, structure, humidity, soil, maintenance | Tests whether the asset can perform without hidden repair shocks |
| Operating and financial | Occupancy, ADR, management fees, tax, reserves, insurance, seasonality | Separates gross rental claims from realistic net investor outcomes |
| Fractional platform | SPV or entity structure, economic rights, escrow, fees, disclosures, exit limits | Shows what investors actually receive and what risks remain |

The goal is not to remove every investment risk. The goal is to identify risks early enough to price them, fix them, disclose them, or reject the project.

## Which Ownership or Investment Structure Are You Using?

Your structure changes the checklist. A direct leasehold buyer, a foreign individual using Hak Pakai, a PT PMA buying through HGB, and a fractional investor on POOOL are not reviewing the same documents.

Indonesia's land framework includes multiple land rights. The Basic Agrarian Law, Law No. 5 of 1960, is the foundation for Indonesian land rights, and Government Regulation No. 18 of 2021 updates rules on land rights, apartment units, and land registration. These are legal source points, not a substitute for counsel review.

### Common structures investors encounter

| Structure | What to verify | Investor caution |
|---|---|---|
| Leasehold | Lease term, extension mechanism, transfer rights, rental rights, registered owner authority | A headline term such as "25 years plus extension" is not enough. The extension formula matters. |
| Hak Pakai | Eligibility, title registration, term, allowed use, transfer rules | Confirm eligibility and use with Indonesian counsel. |
| HGB through PT PMA | Corporate documents, HGB title, investment licensing, tax and operating obligations | The company must be real, compliant, and properly capitalized. |
| Fractional investment | Platform structure, SPV or asset entity, economic rights, disclosures, fees, reporting, exit limits | You need document clarity, not personal land-title assumptions. |
| Nominee arrangement | Any claim that someone else can hold freehold title "for you" | Treat informal nominee ownership as a major red flag until counsel confirms legality and enforceability. |

For POOOL investors, the practical question is not "Can I personally own this land?" It is "What economic rights do I receive, what entity or contract creates those rights, what documents support the project, and what risks are disclosed?"

## How Do You Verify Title, Seller Authority, and Encumbrances?

Title verification is the first hard stop. Before a large deposit, investors should expect independent confirmation that the title exists, the seller has authority, and the land is not blocked by liens, disputes, inheritance issues, tax problems, or conflicting certificates.

For a direct transaction, this usually means working with a qualified PPAT/notary and independent legal counsel to verify the original certificate through the relevant land office process. For fractional investing, the platform should be able to explain which title and authority checks were completed before listing.

### Title and seller checklist

- Original land certificate reviewed, not only a scan.
- Seller identity matches the certificate or corporate authority documents.
- Spousal consent, shareholder approval, or board authority is checked where relevant.
- BPN or land-office verification confirms certificate status.
- Encumbrances, mortgages, court blocks, inheritance disputes, and pending claims are reviewed.
- Survey map and physical boundaries match the real site.
- Taxes or local obligations that could affect transfer or operation are identified.
- The person requesting the deposit has clear authority to receive it.

### Red flags

- The seller refuses independent verification.
- The seller insists you use only their lawyer or notary.
- The original certificate is unavailable.
- The deposit is large, non-refundable, and required before document checks.
- The title holder, seller, and payment recipient are different parties without clear documentation.

POOOL angle: a fractional investor should not need to run every direct-buyer check personally, but they should be able to review a clear due-diligence summary and understand who performed the checks.

## How Do You Check Zoning, Permits, and Rental Legality?

A Bali villa can look finished and still be a poor investment if it cannot legally operate as planned. Zoning, building approval, certificate of functional worthiness, and rental permissions all matter because the investment return usually depends on lawful hospitality operations.

Indonesia's Government Regulation No. 16 of 2021 covers the implementation of the building law framework, including building approval concepts. Business licensing has also changed: BPK notes that Government Regulation No. 28 of 2025 replaced PP No. 5 of 2021 for risk-based business licensing. That matters because rental and hospitality activity may require current OSS or local licensing review, not stale assumptions.

### Permit and use checklist

- Zoning supports the intended residential, hospitality, or mixed use.
- PBG or legacy IMB documentation matches the actual building.
- SLF status is reviewed where applicable.
- Rental or tourism licensing requirements are checked for the intended short-term rental model.
- Building size, number of floors, setback, and use match approved documents.
- Environmental, water, waste, and local community requirements are reviewed.
- Any change of use, renovation, or extension has approval where required.

### Document stack by risk level

| Document or check | Risk if missing | Investor action |
|---|---:|---|
| Title and seller authority | Critical | Do not proceed until independently verified |
| Zoning and land use | High | Confirm intended use before underwriting revenue |
| PBG or legacy IMB | High | Compare approval to built structure |
| SLF where required | Medium to high | Confirm occupancy/functionality status |
| Rental or tourism licensing | High | Verify before relying on rental projections |
| Utility and access documentation | Medium | Check physical and legal access before closing |

## What Physical and Technical Checks Should You Run?

Physical due diligence tests whether the property can operate without expensive surprises. In Bali, this should include more than a quick walkthrough. Climate, humidity, drainage, road access, water reliability, construction quality, and neighboring development can all affect operating performance.

### Site and building checklist

- Legal road access and actual road usability.
- Water source, water rights, storage, pump systems, and dry-season resilience.
- Electricity capacity, backup systems, and panel condition.
- Drainage, flood history, slope, erosion, and retaining walls.
- Soil stability and visible structural movement.
- Roof, waterproofing, humidity, mold, ventilation, plumbing, and electrical quality.
- Pool, wastewater, septic, and greywater systems.
- Maintenance history and warranty documents.
- Neighboring plots, future construction risk, and noise exposure.

For a finished villa, investors should ask for inspection reports, capex estimates, and a maintenance reserve. For a development project, the review should include budget contingencies, construction timeline, contractor history, and milestones tied to fund release.

## How Do You Review the Lease or Purchase Contract?

The contract is where attractive commercial claims become enforceable rights, obligations, or gaps. A lease can sound simple until the extension price, transfer rights, renovation rights, and dispute process are tested.

### Contract checklist

- Lease term, start date, and exact expiry date.
- Extension rights and renewal price formula.
- Transfer, assignment, sale, or sublease rights.
- Rental operation rights and any restrictions.
- Renovation, rebuilding, and improvement ownership.
- Early termination, breach, force majeure, and dispute forum.
- Tax, insurance, banjar, maintenance, and community obligations.
- Currency, payment timing, late fees, and withholding mechanics.
- What happens if permits, access, utilities, or rental licenses change.

The phrase "extendable" is weak unless the mechanism is clear. Investors should know who can extend, when notice must be given, how the renewal price is calculated, and what happens if the parties disagree.

## How Do You Test the ROI Assumptions?

Projected rental income is not the same as net investor return. Bali tourism demand can be strong while a specific villa underperforms because of pricing, location, design, operator quality, maintenance, seasonality, or competition.

BPS Bali reported 6,948,754 direct foreign tourist arrivals in 2025, up 9.72% versus 2024. BPS also reported 492,289 direct foreign visits in February 2026, up 9.23% year over year. These figures support demand context for Bali. They do not prove that any specific villa will hit its target occupancy or investor distribution.

### ROI checklist

- Gross rental revenue assumptions.
- Occupancy by month, not just annual average.
- Average daily rate assumptions and comparable villas.
- Platform, operator, channel, payment, tax, and management fees.
- Cleaning, utilities, repairs, staff, insurance, and local obligations.
- Maintenance reserve and periodic capex.
- FX risk if revenue, expenses, and investor reporting use different currencies.
- Debt service, if the project uses leverage.
- Exit value, sale timing, and liquidity assumptions.

### Gross-to-net return waterfall

```text
Gross rental revenue
- booking platform and payment fees
- operator and property management fees
- staff, cleaning, utilities, and supplies
- taxes, licensing, insurance, and local obligations
- maintenance reserve and capex allowance
- platform or administration fees
= estimated net distributable amount before investor-level tax
```

Every step should be shown before an investor sees a projected return. If the model only shows gross yield, it is incomplete.

## What Should Fractional Investors Check Before Investing Through POOOL?

Fractional investing can simplify access to Bali property, but it does not remove the need for disclosure. The investor should understand the structure before the investment is made.

POOOL's internal whitepaper describes a multi-layered asset onboarding process, including initial screening, title and ownership verification with BPN and notarial review, encumbrance checks, zoning and building-code review, independent appraisal, commercial viability analysis, SPV structuring, approval committee review, ERSA documentation, and ongoing monitoring. That framework should be translated into investor-facing documents for each listed project.

### Fractional investor checklist

- What entity, contract, or arrangement holds or controls the asset?
- What economic rights does the investor receive?
- What documents explain the subscription terms?
- How are investor funds handled before project completion or closing?
- What fees apply at purchase, operation, distribution, and exit?
- Who operates the property, and what is their track record?
- What reporting will investors receive, and how often?
- What assumptions drive the projected performance range?
- What are the lockups, transfer restrictions, or secondary-market limits?
- What happens if the operator, developer, platform, or property underperforms?

The responsible way to read a POOOL listing is to compare the opportunity, documentation, projected performance, and risk profile together. A strong project should make the risk easier to understand, not easier to ignore.

## What Are the Biggest Bali Property Red Flags?

Some issues deserve a pause before any funds are committed. Others may be acceptable if priced, fixed, disclosed, or contractually managed. The difference should be documented.

| Red flag | Severity | Why it matters |
|---|---:|---|
| Guaranteed ROI claims | Critical | Real estate income, resale, occupancy, and FX outcomes cannot be promised |
| Nominee freehold claims | Critical | Informal control over someone else's title can create severe legal risk |
| No independent title check | Critical | Seller-provided scans are not enough |
| Zoning or permit mismatch | High | A rentable-looking villa may not be lawful for intended use |
| Missing PBG/IMB or SLF review | High | Building compliance can affect operation and exit |
| Unclear road access | High | Access problems can damage both use and resale value |
| Vague lease extension terms | High | Future value may depend on an uncertain renewal |
| ROI model excludes fees, tax, reserves, or seasonality | High | Gross revenue can overstate investor outcomes |
| No operator track record | Medium | Hospitality execution drives investor experience |
| No clear exit or secondary-market disclosure | Medium | Fractional real estate may be difficult to sell quickly |

## FAQ

### Can foreigners own freehold property in Bali?

Foreigners should be cautious with personal freehold claims. Indonesia's Basic Agrarian Law and land-rights regulations distinguish between land rights and eligible holders. Any structure involving Hak Milik, Hak Pakai, HGB, leasehold, PT PMA, or fractional economic rights should be reviewed by Indonesian counsel.

### Is leasehold property safe in Bali?

Leasehold can be workable when the title holder has authority, the use is lawful, the lease is properly documented, and extension, transfer, rental, renovation, and dispute terms are clear. The main risk is not the word "leasehold"; it is a weak or incomplete lease.

### What is the most important due diligence check?

Independent verification of title, seller authority, encumbrances, zoning, and permitted use should happen before a large non-refundable payment. If the property cannot be legally owned, leased, built, occupied, or operated as planned, the rest of the investment model is secondary.

### Do I need a lawyer or notary?

For direct acquisition or lease, qualified Indonesian counsel and the proper PPAT/notarial process are important. Fractional investors should still review platform disclosures and legal documents, and they should seek independent advice when the structure, tax treatment, or risk profile is unclear.

### Can due diligence lock in returns?

No. Due diligence can reduce avoidable legal, technical, and operating risk. It cannot lock in occupancy, resale value, rental income, currency movements, liquidity, or investor distributions.
