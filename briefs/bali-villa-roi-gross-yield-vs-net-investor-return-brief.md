## TL;DR Draft

Bali villa ROI should not be judged from gross rental revenue alone. Investors need to model occupancy, ADR, booking fees, management, taxes, utilities, maintenance reserves, capex, currency risk, platform fees, and exit limits before estimating net return. Tourism demand data can support market context, but it does not prove any specific villa's future investor distribution.

## Content Outline

### Introduction

- Hook: the same Bali villa can look like a 12% investment in a brochure and a 5% investment after costs, reserves, taxes, and downtime.
- Define the core problem: ROI is used to mean too many things.
- State that the article is educational, not financial, tax, or legal advice.
- Promise a practical gross-to-net model and fractional-investor checklist.

### H2: What Does Bali Villa ROI Actually Mean?

- Answer-first: ROI can mean gross yield, net yield, cash-on-cash return, IRR, total return with appreciation, or investor distribution yield.
- Cover:
  - Gross rental yield.
  - Net operating yield.
  - Cash-on-cash return.
  - Total return including resale/appreciation.
  - Fractional investor distribution yield.
- Table: ROI term, formula, what it includes, what it excludes.

### H2: Why Gross Rental Revenue Is Not Investor Return

- Answer-first: gross rental revenue is the top line before the costs that make the villa operate.
- Cover:
  - ADR and occupancy.
  - OTA/channel fees.
  - Payment fees.
  - Management fees.
  - Cleaning, staff, utilities, supplies.
  - Taxes, licensing, insurance, local obligations.
  - Maintenance reserves and capex.
- Chart: gross-to-net waterfall.

### H2: What Market Data Can And Cannot Prove

- Answer-first: Bali tourism data can support demand context, but it cannot prove a specific villa's occupancy, ADR, or distributions.
- Cover:
  - BPS Bali February 2026 foreign arrivals.
  - Star-rated and non-star accommodation occupancy rates from BPS as market indicators.
  - Difference between hotel occupancy, villa occupancy, and property-specific performance.
  - Why location, design, reviews, management quality, and regulation matter.
- Compliance note: do not use BPS data as ROI proof.

### H2: What Costs Usually Erode Headline Bali Villa ROI?

- Answer-first: the most common ROI leakage comes from management, channels, maintenance, taxes, utilities, licensing, vacancy, FX, and capex.
- Cover:
  - Property management fees and operating fees.
  - OTA and payment processing.
  - Staff, cleaning, utilities, pool/garden maintenance.
  - Repair reserve and major replacement schedule.
  - Local tax and licensing requirements.
  - Renovation, furniture refresh, and downtime.
  - FX when expenses/revenue/reporting use different currencies.
- Table: cost bucket, how it affects net return, evidence/source needed.

### H2: How Should Investors Stress-Test Occupancy And ADR?

- Answer-first: use at least three cases: downside, base, and upside, and show monthly seasonality instead of one annual average.
- Cover:
  - Occupancy sensitivity.
  - ADR sensitivity.
  - Seasonality and shoulder periods.
  - Oversupply and nearby construction.
  - Review score and management execution.
- Chart: scenario table with downside/base/upside assumptions.
- Note: avoid made-up outputs unless model data is available.

### H2: How Is Fractional Investor Return Different From Property Net Yield?

- Answer-first: a property's net operating income is not automatically the amount distributed to fractional investors.
- Cover:
  - Entity/platform structure, subject to internal legal review.
  - Reserves and distribution policy.
  - Platform/admin fees.
  - Timing of distributions.
  - Investor-level taxes.
  - Lockups, transfer restrictions, secondary liquidity limits.
  - Sale/exit assumptions and valuation uncertainty.
- CTA placement: Stress-test return assumptions.

### H2: What Should A Responsible Bali Villa ROI Model Include?

- Answer-first: a responsible model should show assumptions, sources, cost deductions, reserves, scenarios, and what investors receive after the platform/legal structure.
- Checklist:
  - Purchase or project cost.
  - Furniture/fit-out and capex.
  - ADR and occupancy by month.
  - Gross revenue.
  - Operating costs.
  - Taxes/licensing/insurance.
  - Reserves.
  - Platform/admin fees.
  - Net distributable amount.
  - Exit/sale scenario.
  - Liquidity and FX assumptions.
- Visual: investor underwriting checklist.

### H2: What ROI Claims Should Trigger Extra Due Diligence?

- Answer-first: claims become risky when they are guaranteed, gross-only, unsupported, or dependent on perfect occupancy and resale assumptions.
- Cover:
  - Guaranteed ROI.
  - Guaranteed resale or buyback.
  - No cost breakdown.
  - Hotel/tourism data used as property-level proof.
  - Occupancy above market without evidence.
  - Missing tax/licensing/management assumptions.
  - No downside case.
- Link to due-diligence article.

## FAQ Section

1. What is a realistic ROI for a Bali villa?
   - Direction: answer with ranges only if sourced and caveated; explain that property-level results vary by location, management, licensing, cost base, and occupancy.
2. Is gross yield the same as net return?
   - Direction: no; gross yield is before operating costs and investor/platform deductions.
3. Does high Bali tourism guarantee villa returns?
   - Direction: no; tourism data is demand context, not a property-specific guarantee.
4. What costs should I deduct before estimating net yield?
   - Direction: management, channels, payments, staff, cleaning, utilities, taxes, insurance, licensing, reserves, capex, platform/admin fees.
5. How should fractional investors evaluate ROI?
   - Direction: review project assumptions, fee schedule, distribution policy, risk disclosure, lockups, exit terms, and downside scenarios.
