### H2: How Do You Verify Title, Seller Authority, and Encumbrances?

- Checklist:
  - Original certificate, not only a copy.
  - BPN/land office verification through a qualified PPAT/notary.
  - Seller identity matches certificate.
  - Spousal consent or corporate authority where required.
  - No liens, blocks, court disputes, inheritance disputes, unpaid taxes, or double certificates.
  - Boundary/survey map matches the real site.
- Red flags:
  - Seller refuses independent check.
  - Pressure to pay a large non-refundable deposit before verification.
  - Seller uses only their own lawyer/notary.

### H2: How Do You Check Zoning, Permits, and Rental Legality?

- Checklist:
  - Confirm zoning for intended use.
  - Verify PBG/legacy IMB status.
  - Verify SLF where applicable.
  - Confirm tourism/rental license requirements for short-term rental operations.
  - Check whether the actual building matches approved use, floor area, number of stories, and plot.
- Explain:
  - A villa can look rentable but still fail as an investment if it cannot legally operate as planned.
- Chart idea: "Document stack by risk level" with title, zoning, PBG, SLF, rental license.

### H2: What Physical and Technical Checks Should You Run?

- Checklist:
  - Road access and legal access route.
  - Water source and water rights.
  - Electricity capacity.
  - Drainage and flood risk.
  - Soil stability, slope, erosion, retaining walls.
  - Structural condition, waterproofing, humidity, roof, plumbing, electrical.
  - Neighboring plots and future construction risk.
- Image idea: site inspection photo, villa construction detail, road/access view.

### H2: How Do You Review the Lease or Purchase Contract?

- Checklist:
  - Lease term and extension rights.
  - Renewal price formula.
  - Transfer/sale rights.
  - Sublease and rental rights.
  - Renovation and rebuilding rights.
  - Early termination, breach, force majeure, dispute forum.
  - Tax, maintenance, insurance, and community/banjar obligations.
- Warning:
  - "25 years plus extension" is not enough. The extension mechanism matters.

### H2: How Do You Test the ROI Assumptions?

- Checklist:
  - Gross vs net yield.
  - Occupancy assumptions by season.
  - ADR assumptions and comparable properties.
  - Management fees.
  - Maintenance reserve.
  - Insurance.
  - Taxes and licensing costs.
  - Currency risk.
  - Exit/liquidity assumptions.
- Chart idea: waterfall chart from gross rental revenue to net investor return.
- POOOL angle:
  - Every projected return should be treated as an estimate, not a guarantee.

### H2: What Should Fractional Investors Check Before Investing Through POOOL?

  - Economic rights documentation.
  - Escrow process.
  - Project due diligence summary.
  - Operator/manager identity and role.
  - Risk disclosure.
  - Distribution schedule.
  - Fees.
  - Secondary-market or exit limitations.
- This is the conversion section. It should explain that POOOL simplifies access, but responsible investors still need to review disclosures.

### H2: What Are the Biggest Red Flags?

- Red flags:
  - Guaranteed ROI language.
  - Nominee ownership claims.
  - No original title or no independent BPN check.
  - Zoning mismatch.
  - No PBG/IMB or no SLF where needed.
  - Unclear road access.
  - Lease extension terms left vague.
  - Seller refuses independent lawyer/notary.
  - ROI model excludes fees, tax, maintenance, seasonality, or currency risk.

### FAQ Section

1. **Can foreigners own freehold property in Bali?**
   - Answer carefully: foreigners generally cannot personally own Hak Milik/freehold land. Legal routes depend on structure and should be reviewed by Indonesian counsel.
2. **Is leasehold property safe in Bali?**
   - Answer: it can be workable when properly documented, but the lease terms, extension rights, permitted use, and seller authority matter.
3. **What is the most important due diligence check?**
   - Answer: independent verification of title, ownership, encumbrances, zoning, and permitted use before large payments.
4. **Do I need a lawyer or notary?**
   - Answer: yes for direct acquisition/lease; fractional investors should still review platform disclosures and legal documents.
5. **Can due diligence guarantee returns?**
   - Answer: no. It reduces avoidable legal and operating risk but cannot guarantee occupancy, resale value, exchange rates, or income.
