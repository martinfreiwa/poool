## TL;DR Draft

Fractional property is usually less liquid than public-market investments. Exits may depend on lockup periods, platform transfer rules, buyer demand, pricing uncertainty, final property sale timing, and legal or KYC restrictions. Investors should not assume instant resale, guaranteed buybacks, fixed exit prices, or guaranteed secondary-market demand unless the documents explicitly say so.

## Content Outline

### Introduction (140-170 words)

- Hook: buying a fraction can be easier than exiting one.
- Problem: investors may import public-market liquidity expectations into private fractional property.
- Promise: explain every exit path, its dependencies, and what to review.
- Compliance caveat: educational, not financial/legal/tax advice.

### H2: Is Fractional Property Liquid? (280-330 words)

- Answer-first: generally less liquid than public stocks or listed REITs.
- Define liquidity: speed, price certainty, buyer depth, cost, and transfer rules.
- Compare listed assets vs private property interests.

### H2: What Exit Paths Might Exist? (330-380 words)

- Platform-facilitated secondary sale, if offered.
- Private transfer subject to rules, if allowed.
- Scheduled property sale or project exit.
- Buyback only if explicitly documented and approved.
- No exit until a later event.
- Table: exit path, who controls it, investor dependency, risk.

### H2: What Can Block Or Delay An Exit? (330-380 words)

- Lockups and holding periods.
- KYC/eligibility of buyer.
- Transfer approval.
- No buyer demand.
- Disagreement over price.
- Poor property performance.
- Legal/regulatory/tax constraints.
- Platform process capacity.

### H2: Why Secondary Market Pricing Can Differ From Your Account Value (280-330 words)

- Account value, projected value, book value, and resale price can differ.
- Buyer discounts may reflect risk, timeline, fees, or market conditions.
- Do not imply a floor price unless contractually true.
- Chart: expected resale price factors.

### H2: How Does Fractional Property Liquidity Compare With REITs And Direct Ownership? (300-360 words)

- REITs: public-market liquidity, market volatility, indirect control.
- Direct villa: slow sale process, large-ticket buyer, legal diligence.
- Fractional property: smaller ticket, private transfer constraints, platform rules.
- Timeshare/personal-use products: different intent and rights.

### H2: What Should Investors Ask Before Investing? (360-420 words)

- Is there a lockup?
- Is secondary sale available or only planned?
- Who approves transfers?
- How is resale price set?
- Are fees charged on transfer?
- What happens if no buyer is found?
- What happens if the property is sold?
- What disclosures and reports will investors receive?
- CTA module: Read exit-risk disclosure.

### H2: How Should POOOL Talk About Liquidity Without Overpromising? (280-330 words)

- Use "may be able to", "subject to documents", and "not guaranteed" where accurate.
- Avoid instant-exit, guaranteed buyback, floor-price, fixed timeline, or always-on marketplace language unless legally approved.
- Tie every claim to the actual investor agreement and platform rules.

- Avoid personalized financial advice about whether an investor should hold or sell.
