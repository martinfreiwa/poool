## TL;DR Draft

Leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA/HGB, and fractional exposure solve different Bali property problems. Leasehold is contract-based, Hak Pakai is a registered personal right for eligible foreigners, PT PMA/HGB is a company route with higher compliance, and fractional exposure offers passive economic participation without personal land ownership. Each structure needs legal review and clear risk disclosure.

## FAQ Section

1. Can foreigners own freehold land in Bali?
   - Direction: generally no personal Hak Milik/freehold ownership; cite official law and recommend legal advice.
2. Is leasehold the same as ownership?
   - Direction: no, it is a contractual right to use under agreed terms.
3. Is Hak Pakai better than leasehold?
   - Direction: depends on eligibility, use case, residency, and risk tolerance; avoid universal "better" claims.
4. Do I need a PT PMA to invest in Bali property?
   - Direction: not always; direct commercial ownership is different from passive economic exposure.
5. Does fractional exposure mean I own the land?
   - Direction: explain that rights depend on the legal documents; avoid saying land ownership unless approved.
