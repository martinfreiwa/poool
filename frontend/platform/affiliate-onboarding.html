Legal Agreements

The POOOL Affiliate Program is supported by five core documents. Review each document, then confirm acceptance below.

Affiliate Terms & Conditions

1. Program Structure

The POOOL Affiliate Program is supported by a clear set of core documents covering participation, conduct, approved materials, payouts, and privacy. Each document has a specific role. Together, they define how the program works and what standards apply to affiliate participation.

2. Required Documents

Before activation, you must review and accept:

  • Affiliate Terms & Conditions
  • Affiliate Code of Conduct
  • Approved Marketing Materials Policy
  • Qualified Referral & Payout Policy
  • Affiliate Privacy Notice
3. Activation & Participation

Your affiliate account cannot be activated until all required document acknowledgements are complete. Acceptance of all core documents is a condition of participation.

4. Enforcement & Termination

Affiliate participation is subject to ongoing compliance monitoring. POOOL may issue warnings, require corrections, freeze payouts, suspend affiliates, deny or claw back affected commission, or remove affiliates from the program.

5. Internal Procedures

Some controls, such as complaint handling, investigations, and payout operations, are managed through internal procedures so that the program can be operated consistently and responsibly. Payouts remain subject to the Qualified Referral & Payout Policy and related operational review.

Affiliate Code of Conduct

1. Your Role as a POOOL Affiliate

You may introduce POOOL, share approved materials, use your personal referral link, and direct users to official POOOL information. Your role is referral only — not advice, not sales.

2. What You May Not Do
  • Provide personal investment advice or recommend a specific asset
  • Promise returns, safety, or guaranteed outcomes
  • Negotiate terms, close deals, or handle payments on behalf of users
  • Use private cold outreach (WhatsApp, Telegram, DMs) for affiliate promotion
  • Create custom pitch decks, misleading edits, or unapproved marketing claims
3. Communication Standards

Affiliate communication must stay within general information and compliant referral activity. If a user asks for advice or a recommendation, you must redirect them to official POOOL information, fees, risk disclosures, and documentation.

4. Approved Materials Only

Use approved materials and official POOOL sources. Do not create your own claims, custom pitch decks, or misleading edits. Profile, bio, or story links do not remove the affiliate conduct rules. Once you use your referral link, code, call to action, or referral redirection, the affiliate rules apply in full.

5. Monitoring & Enforcement

Affiliate participation is monitored. Violations may result in: warnings, required corrections, payout freezes, suspension, commission denial or clawback, or permanent removal from the program.

Qualified Referral & Payout Policy

1. What Is a Qualified Referral?

A Qualified Referral means a referred user who is validly attributed, completes required onboarding and KYC, completes a first eligible paid investment, clears the review period, has no disqualifying issue, and passes final payout checks.

2. Tracked → Qualified → Payable

A tracked referral is not yet qualified. A qualified referral is not yet payable. Payout happens only after all final checks are complete. A click, signup, registration, incomplete KYC, or an initiated transaction does not by itself create a payable commission.

3. When Commission Becomes Payable

Commission becomes payable only after the referral is qualified, the review period has cleared, no unresolved issue exists, your payout and tax setup is complete, and the payout is approved for release.

4. Review Period & Holdback

A referral remains provisional for a 30-day review period after the first eligible paid investment before payout can move forward. Payouts may be held or frozen if attribution is disputed, KYC is incomplete, review is still running, fraud or reversal review is open, self-referral or referral-ring activity is suspected, or payout/tax data is incomplete.

5. Reversal & Clawback

If the qualifying event is later reversed or invalidated, or if fraud, self-referral, referral-ring activity, policy breach, or another disqualifying event is found, POOOL may deny, freeze, reverse, offset, or claw back the affected commission.

6. Self-Referral Prohibition

Self-referral is strictly prohibited and leads to disqualification of the referral, removal of commission and tier credit, and further enforcement action.

Approved Marketing Materials Policy

1. Approved Materials

Affiliates must use only POOOL-approved marketing materials. This includes banners, landing page links, social media templates, email copy, and educational content provided through the affiliate dashboard.

2. Prohibited Content
  • Custom pitch decks or presentations not reviewed by POOOL
  • Modified or altered official POOOL materials
  • Content that implies guaranteed returns, principal safety, or risk-free investment
  • Urgency or scarcity messaging not sanctioned by POOOL
  • Use of POOOL trademarks, logos, or brand assets outside approved templates
3. Material Updates

POOOL may update or retire approved materials at any time. Affiliates are responsible for using the most current versions available in the affiliate dashboard. Continued use of retired materials may result in enforcement action.

4. Custom Content Review

If you wish to create original content (blog posts, videos, podcasts) that references POOOL, the content must stay within the boundaries of the Code of Conduct: factual, non-advisory, and directing users to official POOOL information for decision-relevant details.

Affiliate Privacy Notice

1. What Data Is Processed

POOOL may process identity and profile data, onboarding and verification data, referral and attribution data, qualification and payout data, complaint or case data, and technical or audit-log data used to operate the affiliate program.

2. Purpose of Processing

POOOL uses affiliate-program data for onboarding, referral tracking, qualification, payouts, compliance, fraud prevention, support, and program administration.

3. Minimum Data Principle

POOOL aims to process only the data reasonably necessary to operate, protect, review, pay, and govern the program.

4. Access & Retention

Access is limited by role and operational need. POOOL keeps affiliate-program data for as long as reasonably needed for program, audit, legal, tax, fraud-prevention, complaint-handling, and compliance purposes.

5. Security

POOOL aims to protect affiliate-program data through controlled access, secure handling practices, logging, and internal incident-response processes.

6. Your Responsibility

You are responsible for providing accurate and current affiliate, payout, and tax profile information and for updating material changes through the approved process.
