Affiliate Tax Policy

POOOL Affiliate Program — Phase 1 (Controlled Referral Program) · Last Updated: 6 May 2026

Company: PT. POOOL INTERNATIONAL GROUP · Website: www.poool.app

Phase 1 launch document. Some items marked for legal review remain placeholders pending final sign-off. Where this page conflicts with the executed master document, the executed document controls.

1. Purpose

This SOP governs the tax-side handling of affiliate payouts under the POOOL Affiliate Program. Its purpose is to ensure that:

  • affiliate commissions are not released as uncontrolled transfers
  • recipient type is identified before payout
  • tax-sensitive payout cases are routed through the correct review path
  • withholding and reporting checkpoints are documented
  • exception cases are escalated rather than improvised
  • post-payout correction issues are handled through a controlled process
  • and payout records are maintained in an audit-ready format. This SOP is an internal finance and operations control document. It is not intended to determine final legal tax treatment, final withholding positions, treaty outcomes, or jurisdictional conclusions. Those items remain subject to legal/tax review.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to:

  • all affiliate commissions
  • all referral-based payouts
  • all manual commission adjustments that may become payable
  • all payout batches involving Affiliate recipients
  • all payout holds and tax-readiness blocks
  • all reversal, offset, or clawback cases that affect tax or reporting treatment
  • and all post-payout review or correction events involving affiliate payouts. This SOP applies before first payout, during normal payout cycles, and during post-payout review or correction events.

3. Core Rule No affiliate payout may be released unless all of the following are complete: 1. the commission is already Payable under the Qualified Referral & Payout Policy; 2. the

Affiliate’s payout profile is complete; 3. the Affiliate’s tax profile is complete; 4. the recipient has been classified into the correct operational payout/tax category; 5. required withholding/review checkpoints have been completed; 6. no unresolved exception, compliance block, reversal hold, or other payout-blocking issue remains open; 7. finance has approved payout release in the system of record. If any one of these is incomplete, the payout must remain blocked.

4. Operating Principles

4.1 No complete file, no payout Incomplete payout files or tax files must block payout release.

4.2 Classification before release Every payout recipient must be classified before the commission enters the final release stage.

4.3 Finance-controlled withholding/review Withholding/review decisions must be made through a controlled finance workflow, not informal case-by-case improvisation.

4.4 Auditability Finance must be able to evidence:

  • who was paid
  • in what class
  • on what file basis
  • under what withholding/review path
  • with what approval
  • and whether any later correction or hold applied.

4.5 Exception control Where classification, residency, tax status, document sufficiency, beneficiary alignment, or reporting treatment is unclear, the payout must be escalated and held until resolved.

4.6 Dashboard visibility is not release authority A commission appearing in a dashboard or internal status view does not authorize payout release.

5. Definitions

For purposes of this SOP: “Payout Profile” means the required payout-method, beneficiary, and destination data needed to process an affiliate payout. “Tax Profile” means the required tax-related data, declarations, document status, classification indicators, and review fields needed to process an affiliate payout under the approved finance workflow. “Recipient Class” means the operational payout/tax category assigned to the Affiliate recipient for payout-review purposes. “Tax-Ready” means the status indicating that the tax-side review required for payout release has been completed and approved. “Payout-Ready” means the status indicating that the payout-side review required for release has been completed and approved. “Exception Case” means a payout case that cannot be processed under the standard workflow and must be escalated. “Post-Payout Correction” means a controlled finance review triggered after payout release because of classification error, documentation deficiency, withholding/review error, reversal-linked commission issues, or similar material concerns.

7. Recipient Classification Workflow

7.1 Required operational recipient classes For launch, every Affiliate payout recipient must be classified into one of these three operational categories: A. Indonesian Individual Use where the payout recipient is handled as an Indonesian natural person for payout/tax workflow purposes. B. Indonesian Entity Use where the payout recipient is handled as an Indonesian domestic company, business, or other domestic legal/business recipient for payout/tax workflow purposes. C. Foreign Recipient Use where the payout recipient is not handled as an Indonesian domestic individual or Indonesian domestic entity for payout/tax workflow purposes.

7.2 Classification rule The assigned category must be based on the information and documents collected in the payout/tax profile and reviewed by finance under the approved classification workflow.

7.3 No mixed-status payout without review If the Affiliate’s profile contains inconsistent indicators, such as:

  • unclear residency
  • unclear recipient type
  • changed payout beneficiary
  • mismatch between entity/person and tax data
  • mismatch between Affiliate identity and payout beneficiary
  • or incomplete documentation, the payout must not proceed until the category is resolved.

7.4 Change-of-status rule If an Affiliate changes:

  • payout beneficiary
  • country / residency
  • recipient type
  • business structure
  • tax-relevant document set
  • or other tax-relevant data, future payouts must be paused until the file is re-reviewed and re-approved.

8. Required Data Collection Fields

8.1 Core identity / recipient fields Collect at minimum:

  • Affiliate ID
  • full legal name or entity legal name
  • display name if different
  • recipient type selection
  • country / residency
  • contact email
  • contact phone if required internally.

8.2 Payout beneficiary fields Collect at minimum:

  • payout beneficiary name
  • payout method type
  • payout destination details
  • payout currency if applicable
  • beneficiary country
  • beneficiary ownership / relationship confirmation where required.

8.3 Tax-profile fields Collect at minimum:

  • tax classification category
  • individual vs entity status
  • domestic vs foreign status
  • tax identification details where required
  • residency-related declaration where required
  • withholding / treaty review flag where applicable.

8.4 Supporting document fields Collect or record where required:

  • supporting identity / tax document status
  • document received date
  • document expiry date if relevant
  • document review status
  • finance review notes
  • escalation status.

8.5 Operational readiness fields The system of record should capture at minimum:

  • payout-ready status
  • tax-ready status
  • classification-approved status
  • withholding-review status
  • exception flag
  • payout hold flag
  • review owner
  • final approval timestamp
  • payout batch reference if assigned.

8.6 Data sufficiency rule A field marked mandatory for the relevant Recipient Class must be complete before payout may be released.

9. Required Review Workflow

Before Payout Release Step 1 — Commission payable check Finance confirms the commission is already marked Payable under the Qualified Referral & Payout Policy. If not payable, tax review may proceed for readiness purposes, but payout release must not occur. Step 2 — Payout profile check Finance or operations confirms:

  • payout method is complete
  • beneficiary details are complete
  • no payout-block flag exists at face level
  • and no obvious mismatch appears on the file. Step 3 — Recipient classification check Finance assigns or confirms the Recipient Class:
  • Indonesian Individual
  • Indonesian Entity
  • or Foreign Recipient. Step 4 — Tax data sufficiency check Finance confirms that all required tax-related fields and supporting items for that class are present and internally acceptable. Step 5 — Withholding / review checkpoint Finance routes the case through the applicable withholding/review path for that class. This step must be documented even where the result is “no additional withholding action required under the approved framework.” Step 6 — Exception / escalation screening Finance checks whether any exception issue exists, including:
  • unclear classification
  • incomplete data
  • treaty-sensitive issue
  • foreign-recipient uncertainty
  • beneficiary mismatch
  • changed status
  • reversal/clawback overlap
  • compliance block
  • or other material uncertainty. Step 7 — Release approval If all checks pass, finance marks:
  • tax-ready = yes
  • payout-ready = yes
  • release approved = yes. Only then may the commission enter the payout batch for release.

10. Withholding / Review Checkpoints

10.1 General checkpoint rule Before release, every payout must pass a withholding/review checkpoint appropriate to the Recipient Class.

10.2 Indonesian Individual checkpoint Finance must confirm that the payout has been reviewed under the domestic-individual handling path selected by POOOL’s approved tax framework. The baseline supports that this may involve PPh 21 / 26 review depending on final treatment.

10.3 Indonesian Entity checkpoint Finance must confirm that the payout has been reviewed under the domestic-entity handling path selected by POOOL’s approved tax framework. The baseline supports that this may involve PPh 23 review for certain domestic entity/service-type handling.

10.4 Foreign Recipient checkpoint Finance must confirm that the payout has been reviewed under the foreign-recipient handling path selected by POOOL’s approved tax framework. The baseline supports that this may involve PPh 26 review, subject to treaty-sensitive questions.

10.5 Documentation of checkpoint outcome For each payout reviewed, finance should record:

  • Recipient Class
  • review path used
  • whether withholding review was completed
  • whether escalation was triggered
  • whether payout was approved, held, denied, or escalated.

11. Payout Blocking Rules

Payout must remain blocked where any of the following applies:

  • payout profile incomplete
  • tax profile incomplete
  • Recipient Class not confirmed
  • withholding/review not completed
  • finance approval missing
  • exception flag unresolved
  • compliance freeze active
  • clawback hold active
  • reversal issue unresolved
  • related legal/privacy/tax escalation unresolved
  • affiliate not payout-eligible under separate Program controls. There should be no informal override outside an approved escalation and approval path.

12. Exception Handling

12.1 When to open an Exception Case Open an Exception Case if any of the following occurs:

  • unclear or conflicting residency information
  • unclear individual vs entity status
  • foreign recipient with incomplete supporting data
  • mismatch between Affiliate profile and payout beneficiary
  • changed tax status after prior approval
  • repeated resubmission failures
  • payout already scheduled before tax review completion
  • post-payout correction issue
  • treaty-sensitive question
  • unusual cross-border structure
  • or any other case finance cannot process under the standard workflow.

12.2 Exception handling steps 1. place payout on hold; 2. open Exception Case ID; 3. record the unresolved issue and reason code; 4. request additional data/documents if needed; 5. escalate to legal/tax reviewer if required; 6. document the final resolution; 7. only then release, continue hold, deny payout, or route into correction handling.

12.3 No forced payout rule Commercial pressure, Affiliate status, payout age, or batch timing must not force release where the file is not clean.

13. Post-Payout Corrections

If a payout is later found to involve:

  • incorrect classification
  • missing tax documentation
  • withholding/review error
  • reversal-linked commission basis
  • post-release discovery of beneficiary mismatch
  • or other material tax/payout issue, finance must open a Post-Payout Correction case and determine whether:
  • no action is needed
  • future payout should be blocked
  • correction entry is needed
  • offset should be applied
  • clawback review should be initiated
  • or legal/tax escalation is required. Paid status does not by itself prevent later correction where material error or a linked disqualifying issue is later identified.

14. Workflow by Recipient Class

14.1 Indonesian Individual Workflow 1. collect individual payout and tax fields; 2. verify data completeness; 3. confirm classification as Indonesian Individual; 4. route through domestic-individual withholding/review checkpoint; 5. resolve any missing-data or mismatch issue; 6. mark tax-ready if approved; 7. allow payout release only after full finance approval.

14.2 Indonesian Entity Workflow 1. collect entity payout and tax fields; 2. verify legal entity and beneficiary data consistency; 3. confirm classification as Indonesian Entity; 4. route through domestic-entity withholding/review checkpoint; 5. resolve any entity-document or classification issue; 6. mark tax-ready if approved; 7. allow payout release only after full finance approval.

14.3 Foreign Recipient Workflow 1. collect foreign-recipient payout and residency-related data; 2. verify completeness of foreign-recipient profile; 3. confirm classification as Foreign Recipient; 4. route through foreign-recipient withholding/review checkpoint; 5. assess whether treaty-sensitive or cross-border escalation is required; 6. hold payout until resolved where required; 7. mark tax-ready only after review is complete.

15. Approval Matrix

POOOL should maintain an internal approval matrix covering at minimum:

  • standard domestic-individual payout approvals
  • standard domestic-entity payout approvals
  • standard foreign-recipient payout approvals
  • Exception Cases
  • Post-Payout Corrections
  • clawback / tax-overlap cases. At minimum, the system should identify:
  • preparer
  • reviewer
  • approver
  • escalation owner
  • final release owner.

16. Reporting Readiness and Period-End Controls

16.1 Core readiness objective Finance must be able to produce a clean year-end or reporting-period view of affiliate payouts by Recipient Class and review status.

16.2 Minimum reporting readiness checklist Before year-end close or reporting cycle close, confirm:

  • all paid Affiliates are classified
  • all payout files are complete
  • all blocked payouts are tagged with reason codes
  • all foreign-recipient cases are clearly identified
  • all Exception Cases are clearly marked open or closed
  • all payout registers reconcile to the commission ledger
  • all withheld/reviewed cases are identifiable
  • all reversal and clawback cases are separately tagged
  • supporting files are retrievable
  • finance sign-off records are complete.

16.3 Reporting files Maintain at minimum:

  • payout register
  • Recipient Class list
  • tax-ready status report
  • Exception Case report
  • blocked-payout report
  • reversal/clawback report
  • supporting-document availability status.

16.4 Audit-readiness rule Finance should be able to answer, for each paid Affiliate:

  • who was paid
  • in what class
  • under what review path
  • on what approval basis
  • whether any later correction, hold, or recovery issue applied.

17. Recordkeeping and Evidence Standards

For each payout reviewed under this SOP, retain:

  • Affiliate ID
  • Recipient Class
  • payout beneficiary details
  • tax-data completeness record
  • withholding/review checkpoint record
  • approval timestamps
  • Exception Case notes if any
  • payout batch reference
  • and any later correction, hold, offset, or clawback note. Retention must follow POOOL’s approved retention framework and any applicable legal, tax, audit, complaint, fraud, or investigation hold rules.

18. System and Control Requirements

Before launch, Product / Engineering must support:

  • payout profile fields
  • tax profile fields
  • Recipient Class fields
  • tax-ready status
  • payout-ready status
  • payout-block status
  • exception flag
  • approval log
  • exportable audit trail
  • and finance ability to prevent release where tax review is incomplete.

19. Internal Escalation Triggers Mandatory escalation should occur where

:

  • Recipient Class is unclear
  • foreign-recipient treatment is unclear
  • supporting tax/residency data is inconsistent
  • payout beneficiary mismatch exists
  • treaty issue may exist
  • payout was scheduled before tax clearance
  • repeated incomplete-file cases occur
  • year-end/reporting treatment is uncertain
  • or the case overlaps with a compliance freeze or payout dispute.

20. Review and Update

This SOP should be reviewed:

  • before go-live
  • after pilot-stage payout learnings
  • when the payout provider or payout process changes
  • when Recipient Classes expand
  • when tax-handling assumptions change
  • and before full-scale payout automation. Summary pages, dashboard notices, onboarding copy, and other user-facing convenience text do not amend this SOP unless POOOL expressly states otherwise in writing.
