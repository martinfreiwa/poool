### Competitor Gaps

1. Fractional-investor due diligence is underserved. Most pages assume the reader is buying or leasing directly.
2. Direct ownership comparisons rarely explain economic rights, SPVs, escrow, distributions, secondary liquidity, or platform/operator risk.
3. ROI pages are commercially strong but often need clearer separation of gross revenue, operating costs, taxes, maintenance reserves, management fees, platform fees, financing, currency, and net investor return.
4. Few competitor pages show a neutral "which path fits you" framework across direct leasehold, Hak Pakai, PT PMA, REIT-style exposure, tokenized/fractional exposure, and doing nothing.
5. Developer and seller content remains thin compared with investor-facing content.

Publish blockers:

- Any statement implying guaranteed ROI, guaranteed liquidity, protected principal, risk-free exposure, or assured passive income.
- Any claim that foreigners can personally own Hak Milik/freehold land in Indonesia.
- Any OJK, licensing, regulated-status, ERSA enforceability, escrow protection, or SPV investor-rights claim without internal legal approval.
- Any ROI/yield figure that does not distinguish gross revenue from net investor return and list core deductions.
