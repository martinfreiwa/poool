Disclaimer and Important Information
This document contains critical legal and operational information regarding the POOOL platform. It is not an offer to sell or a
solicitation of an offer to buy any securities or financial products. All investment activities are governed by the laws and
regulations of the Republic of Indonesia.
- Legal Structure and Nature of Investment
For the avoidance of any doubt, all references to "ownership," "fractional ownership," or "shares" on the POOOL platform and in
associated documents must be strictly understood as a subscription to an Economic Rights Subscription Agreement
(ERSA). This agreement grants the investor contractual economic rights, which are digitally represented by an NFT-based
certificate. These rights include, but are not limited to, the right to distributions, a share of profits from a Special Purpose
Vehicle (SPV), and participation in proceeds upon asset disposal.
● No Direct Legal Title: Investors on the POOOL platform do not acquire direct legal title to land, property, or company
equity. The underlying asset is held by a bankruptcy-remote SPV, which is the legal owner.
- Compliance with Indonesian Law
POOOL is committed to full compliance with Indonesian law, including regulations from the Financial Services Authority (OJK)
and Bank Indonesia (BI).
● Financial Transactions: All transactions, including subscription payments, distribution of profits, and secondary
transfers, are conducted exclusively in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). These transactions are facilitated through licensed
Indonesian banks or registered Payment Service Providers (PSPs) in accordance with BI regulations.
● Settlement Protocol: No cross-border, foreign currency, or crypto-native settlements are permitted or facilitated
within the platform. The use of "blockchain" technology (specifically Polygon) is limited to serving as a secure registry
for recording economic rights. All underlying financial flows remain within the Indonesian financial system.
● Land Ownership Restrictions: The SPV structure ensures compliance with Indonesian land ownership laws,
including restrictions on foreign ownership. Foreign investors acquire beneficial economic rights in the Indonesian
SPV, which itself holds the property under a permitted title (e.g., Hak Guna Bangunan (HGB), Hak Pakai, or Strata
## Title).
- Regulatory Oversight: OJK Regulatory Sandbox
POOOL operates under the direct supervision of the OJK as a Digital Financial Innovation (DFI) service within the OJK
Regulatory Sandbox. This participation demonstrates our commitment to a controlled, compliant, and transparent operational
model.
● Regulatory Framework: Our platform adheres to the principles and provisions of:

- OJK Regulation No. 13/2018 on Digital Financial Innovation in the Financial Services Sector.
- OJK Regulation No. 57/2020 on Equity Crowdfunding (referenced by analogy).
- OJK Regulation No. 8/2023 regarding Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing (AML/CFT)
obligations.

● Mandatory Reporting: We provide regular, mandatory reports to the OJK, which include metrics on investor
onboarding, Assets Under Management (AUM), transaction volumes, customer complaints, Suspicious Transaction
Reports (STRs), and system performance.
- Consumer Protection and Disclosures
POOOL places the highest priority on investor protection and transparency, in line with OJK's consumer protection regulations.
● Comprehensive Disclosures: Each investment offering is accompanied by:

- A Key Facts Statement (KFS).
- A clear and comprehensive list of Risk Factors.
- A mandatory cooling-off period of at least 2 x 24 hours.
- Explicit disclosure of all fees, including a primary markup (10-20%), secondary transaction fees (0.5-1%), and a
profit-based exit fee (5%).

● Investor Suitability: We enforce rigorous investor suitability checks and, where applicable, annual investment limits
to ensure that investment aligns with the investor's financial capacity and risk profile.

● Secondary Market: The secondary market operates as a bulletin/OTC board accessible only to Know Your
Customer (KYC)-verified investors. It is crucial to understand that there is no guarantee of liquidity for fractional
interests.
- Technology and Security
Our technology architecture is built to serve as a secure and compliant registry.
● Blockchain Functionality: The use of blockchain is strictly limited to record-keeping and registry functions. The
NFTs serve solely as digital certificates linked to the underlying ERSA contracts.
● Financial & Asset Custody: Custody of funds, payment settlement, and asset management are fully handled by
licensed financial institutions within Indonesia.
● Information Security: Our IT system is developed to align with international best practices and Indonesian
regulations, including ISO/IEC 27001, the Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL), and the OJK’s IT Risk Management
## Framework.

Driving Innovative Tokenization with a Robust Legal
Framework and Clear Regulatory Commitment
POOOL leverages a sophisticated legal and technological framework designed to provide
secure and compliant fractional real estate investment in Indonesia. Our approach integrates
asset tokenization via NFT-based digital certificates with robust traditional legal structures,
specifically Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs).

● Dedicated SPV Structure: For each property offering, a dedicated,
bankruptcy-remote Indonesian legal entity (Perseroan Terbatas - PT) is established
to serve as the SPV. This SPV holds the legal title to the underlying real estate asset,
ensuring legal clarity and segregation of assets.

● Economic Rights Subscription Agreement (ERSA): Investors subscribe to an
ERSA, which grants them contractual economic rights to the property's income and
proceeds, rather than direct equity ownership in the SPV. These economic rights are
digitally represented by unique NFT-based certificates issued to the investors'
whitelisted digital wallets.

● Escrow-Based Fund Management and Investor Safeguard: Investor funds are
deposited into a licensed escrow account, managed by a reputable financial
institution. These funds are held in escrow until all predefined conditions precedent
are met, which include:

○ Full funding of the target investment amount.

○ Completion of all Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering
(AML) verifications for investors.

○ Execution of the definitive property sale and purchase agreement.
Only upon the satisfaction of all these conditions and the readiness to close
the property acquisition, will the escrowed capital be released to the SPV to
finalize the purchase of the underlying real estate asset. This rigorous
process ensures that investor funds are fully protected throughout the
fundraising phase and are never released until the asset acquisition is
assured, thereby mitigating risks associated with failed projects.

● Immutable Digital Evidence and Legal Synchronization: Following the successful
acquisition of the asset by the SPV, POOOL issues NFT-based Investment
Certificates. These digital certificates mirror the legally maintained investor register of
the SPV, which is overseen by a public notary. This hybrid approach creates an
immutable digital record on the blockchain of the investor's proportional beneficial
rights while ensuring that these rights are legally recognized and enforceable under
Indonesian law through the SPV structure and notarial records. The blockchain
ledger provides an auditable trail, enhancing transparency and investor confidence.

## 2. Issuer Journey: Accessing Efficient Capital Raising

● Asset Submission and Initial Review: Interested Issuers submit their property for
consideration, providing all necessary documentation, including legal title deeds,
comprehensive valuation reports, and relevant permits.

● Intensive Due Diligence on Issuer and Asset: POOOL conducts a thorough,
multi-faceted due diligence process:

○ Legal Verification: Scrupulous verification of the property’s legal title,
involving checks with the Indonesian land registry for any liens,
encumbrances, or disputes.

○ Appraisal Validation: Independent validation of property appraisals to
ensure fair market value.

○ Issuer Background Checks: Rigorous background checks on the Issuer to
confirm legitimacy, financial standing, and compliance with all regulatory and
platform-specific criteria (e.g., absence from prohibited sectors). Only assets
and Issuers that successfully pass these stringent checks are accepted.

● Offering Structuring and SPV Creation: POOOL facilitates the establishment of a
dedicated SPV (if not already existing) for the asset and expertly structures the
offering terms, ensuring alignment with OJK regulations and market best practices.

● Conditional Agreement and Investor Fund Safeguarding: The Issuer executes a
conditional Sale and Purchase Agreement (SPA) or a similar binding contract (e.g.,
Perjanjian Pengikatan Jual Beli - PPJB) with the SPV. This agreement is strictly
contingent upon the successful funding of the offering and the fulfillment of other
predefined conditions precedent. Crucially, during the fundraising period, the Issuer
has no access to investor funds; all monies remain securely in the licensed escrow

account. Property title transfer to the SPV only occurs at the successful closing of the
transaction, upon full payment. This robust mechanism provides absolute assurance
to investors that their funds are safeguarded and will only be utilized for the intended
asset acquisition, with a clear return mechanism in the event of deal failure.

## Legal Structure
POOOL has designed its legal structure to ensure that the innovative tokenization model fits
within Indonesia’s regulatory framework and provides strong protection of investor rights.
The core elements of this legal structure are the use of Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)
for asset ownership, the Economic Rights Subscription Agreement (ERSA) for investor
subscriptions, and the issuance of NFT-based investment certificates that represent
investors’ beneficial interests. All these elements work together under the oversight of legal
professionals (notaries, lawyers) to create a compliant and enforceable framework for
fractional real estate investment.

legal verification with the National Land Agency (BPN) and notarial checks to confirm valid
title, absence of liens, and compliance with zoning and environmental regulations. Issuer
representatives are screened for litigation history, blacklist status, and regulatory eligibility,
while independent appraisal reports are obtained to validate asset valuations and assess

governance checks extend to audited accounts, debt obligations, and the issuer’s track

including full funding, investor KYC clearance, and regulatory approval. All issuers covenant

cooperate with compliance reviews. Final approval for listing requires sign-off by POOOL’s
internal Listing Approval Committee — comprising Legal, Compliance, Risk, and Operations

Agreement (ERSA). Importantly, issuer obligations continue post-listing, with mandatory
property management updates, financial reporting, and covenant compliance monitored on

Asset Valuation and Independence of Appraisal: All property assets listed on POOOL
undergo a formal appraisal to ensure fairness and transparency for investors. Independent
licensed valuers (KJPP – Kantor Jasa Penilai Publik) are engaged to conduct third-party

Investors subscribe based on the appraised fair market value at the time of listing. During
the investment period, there is no daily or continuous revaluation of the asset, as this is not a
tradable security fund. Exit value is realized only in two scenarios:
- Property sale by the SPV – when the asset is divested, investors receive their
proportional share of net proceeds (capital appreciation or loss, plus any income).

- Secondary transfer – investors may resell their ERSA-based NFT fractions via the
platform’s OTC bulletin board to other verified investors at a mutually agreed price.

Step 2. Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) Structure: Each property asset offered on POOOL
is held through a dedicated SPV established solely for that asset. The SPV is typically a
newly incorporated Indonesian limited liability company (Perseroan Terbatas, PT) whose
constitution and articles of association restrict it to owning and managing the specific
underlying property . This “one asset, one SPV” approach ring-fences the asset and
isolates it from any risks unrelated to that property. Investors who participate in an offering
do not directly buy shares of the SPV during the fundraising; instead, they enter into an
ERSA which gives them a contractual right to the economic benefits of the SPV/asset once
the asset is acquired. Upon successful completion of funding and the property purchase, the
SPV’s ownership (shares) is structured to be held by a trustee or nominee for the benefit of
the investors as per the subscription agreements, or the investors may be formally recorded
in the SPV’s share registry if and as allowed by OJK in the sandbox. In either case, the
beneficial interest of each investor in the SPV’s assets is recognized and documented.
Using an SPV per asset offers legal clarity: the investor community of a given property has a
direct claim on that property’s performance (rental income, eventual sale proceeds) through
the SPV, and that SPV is bankruptcy-remote from POOOL’s corporate entities or any other
projects . If POOOL (the platform operator) were ever to face insolvency, the assets held in
each SPV would remain separate and protected, since the SPV is a distinct legal entity. This
structure is a common finance mechanism for asset-backed investments and provides an
additional layer of security for investors, as their investment is tied to a specific asset and not
comingled with the platform’s general business or other assets . All property acquisitions by
the SPV utilize the appropriate Indonesian land ownership instruments (e.g., Hak Guna
Bangunan (HGB) for leasehold interests, Hak Milik if available for certain properties, Hak
Pakai, or strata title for condominium units, depending on the asset type) and are executed
via notarized deeds. The SPV holds the land title on trust for the investors, and legal
opinions are obtained to ensure the validity of the ownership structure, with these opinions
summarized in the offering documents .
Step 3. Economic Rights Subscription Agreement (ERSA): The ERSA is the central
contract between the investor and the platform/SPV that defines the investor’s rights. Under
the ERSA, an investor subscribes to a certain economic interest in the SPV (which
corresponds to a fraction of the property asset to be acquired) in exchange for their
investment funds. Importantly, during the initial offering, this does not constitute an issuance
of shares or securities; rather, it is a contractual commitment that converts into a beneficial
ownership right once the property is purchased by the SPV . In essence, the ERSA says: if
the target property is successfully acquired, the investor will be entitled to a proportional
share of the economic benefits (rental income, net proceeds on sale) from that asset, and
this entitlement will be evidenced by a digital token (NFT certificate) and reflected in the legal
records of the SPV. If the deal fails to close (e.g., funding target not met or conditions not
satisfied), the ERSA provides that the subscription is canceled and the funds are returned to
the investor from escrow . This structure has been deliberately designed to avoid triggering
Indonesia’s securities laws during the fundraising phase. Since investors are not buying a
tradable security upfront (no shares or bonds are issued at the point of subscription), and

their funds remain in escrow subject to conditions, the arrangement is more akin to a
conditional sale contract or crowdfunding-style subscription rather than a public
offering of securities. POOOL has taken legal guidance to ensure that the ERSA instrument
and process do not violate prevailing regulations (such as OJK’s capital market rules) –
essentially treating the fractional offering as an innovative financial contract under sandbox
supervision, rather than a classical equity offering. The legal classification of the
fractional instrument will ultimately be determined by OJK in the course of the sandbox;
POOOL does not unilaterally label it a certain way (security or otherwise) without regulatory
consent . However, by structure, it is a beneficial entitlement in an asset-holding vehicle,
and current indications (under new legislation) are that such tokens will be regulated as
Digital Financial Assets (DFA) rather than traditional securities . This means they would fall
under a forthcoming digital asset regulatory framework (with OJK oversight as mandated by
Indonesia’s 2023 financial sector reform law) as opposed to needing a securities issuance
license. POOOL’s approach is to remain fully transparent about this structure – making clear
to investors that they are obtaining economic rights via contract (and subsequently via token)
and not shareholder rights in the conventional sense. Investors do not obtain voting stock in
the SPV by default; major decisions (like selling the property) are typically pre-agreed or, if
investor input is needed, conducted via token-holder voting mechanisms as described in the
offering terms. This careful structuring is aimed at complying with current laws while OJK
refines the appropriate categorization for tokenized assets. By the end of the sandbox
period, POOOL expects to align with any new licensing that OJK introduces for platforms
issuing such digital asset investment certificates.
Step 4. NFT-Based Investment Certificates: After the property is acquired by the SPV at
closing, POOOL issues non-fungible token (NFT) certificates to each investor,
corresponding to their fraction of the asset/SPV ownership . Each NFT is a unique token
recorded on a public blockchain (currently Polygon network, chosen for its scalability and
low transaction costs) and contains or references metadata about the investment – such as
the asset name, the SPV identifier, the investor’s wallet address, and the number of fractions
or percentage interest it represents . These NFTs serve as the digital mirror of the SPV’s
investor register. POOOL maintains an official register of investors through traditional
means (with a notary overseeing the issuance of any formal certificates or annotations of the
cap table) and ensures that the on-chain tokens map 1:1 to that register . In practical terms,
holding the NFT entitles the holder to the benefits defined in the ERSA: it is evidence of
one’s beneficial ownership slice of the SPV’s asset . The NFT itself is secured by blockchain
technology, meaning it cannot be tampered with or counterfeited, and any transfer of the
token is transparently logged. To prevent unauthorized transfers (which could complicate the
legal register), POOOL whitelists investor wallet addresses – tokens can only be held or
moved to wallets that have been KYC-verified and linked to the platform’s system. This
prevents, for example, someone from selling the token on an open market to an unknown
party; all secondary transfers must go through the platform’s processes to ensure the legal
owner register is updated and the buyer is eligible (in compliance with any investor limits or
qualifications) . By integrating the NFT with legal contracts, POOOL ensures that even
though ownership is represented digitally, it remains enforceable in court: the notarial
records and SPV acknowledgment mean an NFT holder has a legally recognized claim on
the underlying asset’s economics. In essence, the NFT certificate is an innovative form of a
securities certificate or investment contract, adapted for the blockchain era. It gives
tech-powered benefits (transparency, tradability, proof of ownership) while retaining legal

robustness through parallel off-chain documentation . This dual-record system provides
redundancy: even if the blockchain were compromised, the legal records can restore
investor rights, and vice versa, if there’s any discrepancy, the blockchain record provides an
audit trail to investigate.
Step 5. Legal Oversight and Compliance: POOOL employs external legal counsel and
notaries to oversee the critical legal processes in each offering. Before an offering is
launched, a comprehensive legal due diligence is conducted. This covers verifying the
property’s title status, checking for any encumbrances or claims, ensuring the seller has the
right to sell, and confirming that the transaction structure (SPA, SPV setup, escrow
conditions) is sound and compliant with Indonesian law . A licensed notary public is engaged
to handle or review the SPV incorporation and to notarize key documents, including (if
applicable) the issuance of fractional ownership certificates or the investment agreements.
During closing, the notary oversees the execution of the sale and purchase deed for the
property and can also attest to the issuance of the digital certificates (the NFT records) as a
true representation of the ownership fractions. In some cases, a notarial deed of collective
investment or a trustee arrangement may be used to further formalize the link between the
token holders and the SPV’s shares. The result is that each investor’s claim is backed by
contract law and recorded by a trusted third-party (notary), providing assurance beyond the
platform’s own representations . POOOL also ensures that ongoing legal compliance is
maintained: for example, the SPV will be audited annually, and any rental income or asset
sale will be conducted through legal channels with proper tax withholdings. All necessary
licenses for the SPV’s activities (such as rental permits if the property is rented out, or
compliance with strata title laws if the property is part of a condominium) are kept up to date.
By marrying cutting-edge blockchain tokenization with Indonesia’s established legal
practices (company law, land law, notarial system), POOOL’s structure is both innovative
and grounded. This approach has been presented to regulators during sandbox discussions
to demonstrate that while the form is new (NFT tokens), the substance – fractional
ownership via a company and contracts – is an extension of familiar legal concepts, thus
ensuring regulatory comfort and investor protection.

## Funds Flow Protocol & Investor Fund Safeguarding
POOOL's funds flow protocol is meticulously designed to protect investor funds and ensure
full compliance with OJK and Bank Indonesia regulations, particularly those concerning the
separation and security of client assets. This process is built upon an escrow-controlled
mechanism, guaranteeing that investor capital is safeguarded by an independent, regulated
third party until all legal conditions for a transaction are met.
- Investor Subscription and Escrow Deposit
Upon selecting a real estate offering, investors execute a digital Economic Rights
Subscription Agreement (ERSA), which outlines the conditional nature of their investment.
Funds are then transferred into a segregated escrow account designated exclusively for
that specific offering. POOOL partners with licensed, reputable financial
institutions—domestic banks or licensed payment service providers—to act as the escrow
agent.
Each offering is assigned a unique escrow sub-account to ensure strict fund segregation
by project. Funds are deposited via approved payment methods, with all transactions
directed to this trust account. Critically, these funds are not commingled with POOOL's or
the issuer's operational accounts. This structure ensures that neither POOOL nor the issuer
can unilaterally access or use the funds; their release is subject to predefined conditions and

the independent oversight of the escrow agent. The escrow agent confirms the deposit, and
the investor's subscription status on the platform is updated accordingly.
- Conditional Holding Period and Funds Safekeeping
Investor funds remain in escrow during the fundraising period until all conditions precedent
(CPs) are fulfilled. Key CPs include:
● Minimum Funding Target: The offering must achieve its minimum fundraising
threshold.
● KYC/AML Clearance: All investor KYC and AML checks must be successfully
completed.
● Legal Documentation: All final legal agreements, such as the Sale and Purchase
Agreement (SPA) for the property, must be finalized and ready for execution.
● Regulatory Approvals: Any required regulatory approvals or notifications must be
received.
This phase includes a mandatory cooling-off period, as per consumer protection
guidelines, which allows investors a limited time (e.g., 48 hours) to reconsider and cancel
their investment for a full refund. If a project fails to meet its CPs, such as not reaching the
funding target, the offering is aborted, and all funds are fully refunded from the escrow
account back to the originating investors.
- Closing, SPV Acquisition, and Funds Release
Once all CPs are satisfied, the Platform, the escrow agent, and a public notary coordinate
the closing. The escrowed funds are then released to the Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)'s
account to consummate the property purchase. The notary oversees the transfer of the
purchase price to the seller and the transfer of the property title to the SPV.
Simultaneously, ancillary costs, taxes, and POOOL's platform fee are disbursed from the
escrow as per the pre-agreed closing statement. This mechanism ensures that POOOL's
revenue is earned only upon the successful closing of a project. The release of funds is
subject to a dual-control "maker-checker" system, requiring authorization from both a
POOOL financial officer and the escrow agent to prevent any unauthorized fund movement.
This is a critical internal control measure that aligns with the principles of good corporate
governance and risk management.

- Token Issuance and Post-Closing
After the property title is officially transferred to the SPV, POOOL proceeds with the issuance
of NFT investment tokens. Each investor receives the precise number of tokens
corresponding to their fractional ownership. These tokens are delivered to the investor's
digital wallet within the Platform, and the SPV's official ownership registry is updated to
reflect each investor’s beneficial interest.
At this point, investors can begin to enjoy the economic benefits of their ownership, such as
receiving their proportional share of rental income. They may also trade their tokens on the
secondary marketplace, subject to the Platform's rules.

- Abort Scenario and Investor Refund
Should a deal be aborted for any reason prior to closing, the escrow agent will initiate a full
refund of all investor funds (minus any non-recoverable, clearly disclosed third-party
transaction fees). Refunds are processed back to the original source account within a
specified business timeframe, as detailed in the Offering Document. This process is
designed to ensure that investors do not lose capital on failed offerings. POOOL does not
charge any fees in an aborted scenario, reinforcing our commitment to protecting investor
capital.















## Compliance Alignment
As a fintech platform operating in a novel space, POOOL places compliance at the forefront
of its operations. The business model has been crafted to align with OJK’s regulatory
expectations and requirements, ensuring that innovation does not come at the expense of
consumer protection or financial stability. Below, we outline how POOOL complies with
relevant regulations and supervisory guidelines, especially under the framework of the OJK
Regulatory Sandbox, and how we plan to transition to full compliance under future
regulations for digital assets.
● Regulatory Sandbox Participation: POOOL has been admitted into OJK’s
regulatory sandbox for innovative financial services, which indicates regulatory
approval to test our model with certain limitations. Within the sandbox, we adhere to
a defined scope and scale of operations as agreed with OJK . For example, in the
initial phase, the number of users and total funds raised may be capped (e.g., a pilot
with up to 1,000 investors and IDR X billion in assets) to control risk exposure .
Transaction sizes per investor might also be limited in line with regulatory guidance
(similar to how equity crowdfunding limits investment per investor based on income) .
We have built-in mechanisms to enforce such limits – e.g., the platform can impose a
maximum investment amount for each user according to their verified income
bracket, following the principles of OJK’s crowdfunding regulations . The sandbox
also requires clear Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and monitoring of
outcomes. POOOL, in coordination with OJK, tracks KPIs such as number of new
and active investors, total funds raised (AUM), number of deals funded, incidence of
any defaults or refunds, secondary market transaction volume, customer complaint
statistics, and system uptime/security incident occurrences  . We submit periodic
reports (monthly or as requested) to OJK detailing these KPIs and any issues or
incidents, ensuring transparency about our operations. Additionally, any deviations
from expected performance or risk events (e.g., a fraud attempt or a significant tech
outage) are promptly reported to OJK in accordance with sandbox protocols. By
operating in the sandbox, POOOL demonstrates a proactive approach to regulatory
compliance – we welcome regulator input and oversight during this trial phase
and are prepared to adjust features or controls as directed to meet regulatory comfort
levels . The sandbox phase is also being used to refine the legal classification of our
product (the fractional tokens) and to determine the appropriate long-term licensing
category.

● Licensing Trajectory (Digital Financial Assets): Indonesia’s regulatory landscape
for fintech and digital assets is evolving. POOOL’s business model of tokenized real
estate touches on elements of capital markets, crowdfunding, and crypto-assets. We
are closely following the latest regulations, including POJK No.13/2018 (which
established the fintech sandbox), POJK No.57/2020 (equity crowdfunding rules), and
new regulations under the authority given by the 2023 financial reform law (UU
P2SK) that transfers crypto-asset oversight to OJK by 2025 . Based on OJK’s
indications, fractional asset tokens like ours will likely fall under the category of
Digital Financial Assets (DFA), which will be regulated by OJK (taking over from
BAPPEBTI’s previous purview) . Our strategy is to fully comply with whatever

permanent licensing framework OJK establishes post-sandbox. The target is that
after successful sandbox testing and upon OJK’s recommendation, POOOL will
obtain a permanent license/registration as a Digital Asset Operator or similar,
allowing it to operate openly under defined regulations . We are preparing for this by
ensuring all aspects of our business – capitalization, governance, risk management,
IT security, reporting – meet the standards OJK will require for a licensed entity. For
example, we maintain capital reserves that meet or exceed any minimum capital
requirements OJK might impose on DFA platforms (drawing insight from existing OJK
rules for fintech funding platforms, etc.) . We anticipate needing to transition from the
sandbox no later than 2025, aligning with OJK’s timeline to take full control of the
sector. POOOL’s early engagement and compliance efforts position us favorably to
be among the first wave of fully licensed tokenization platforms in Indonesia,
which will provide long-term regulatory certainty for the business and its users .

● Know-Your-Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML): POOOL has
implemented rigorous KYC/AML procedures in accordance with Indonesian
regulations and international best practices. Every user (investor or issuer) must go
through a comprehensive KYC verification during onboarding . This involves
collecting official ID (e.g. KTP or passport), proof of address, and user selfies for
biometric verification, which we process via an approved electronic KYC service
provider for efficiency and accuracy . The platform cross-checks user information
against government databases when possible and screens all users against
sanctions lists, terrorism finance watchlists, and PEP (Politically Exposed Persons)
lists . Only individuals and entities that pass verification and screening are allowed to
invest; any flagged cases undergo Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) or are rejected
outright. POOOL’s AML program is designed to comply with OJK Regulation No.
8/2023 which extends AML/CFT obligations to fintech operators . We have internal
policies and an AML officer in charge of monitoring transactions on the platform. The
system automatically monitors for suspicious patterns – for instance, multiple
accounts funding from the same source, unusual large transactions relative to a
user’s profile, rapid in-and-out movements of funds, etc. – and generates alerts. We
file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) and Cash Transaction Reports
(CTRs) to PPATK (Indonesia’s Financial Intelligence Unit) as required by law . In
practice, because our primary market flows are escrow-based and the secondary
market (if enabled) is within a closed ecosystem, the risk of anonymous money flows
is limited; however, we remain vigilant for any attempt to use the platform for illicit
purposes. Accounts associated with any suspicious activity can be frozen pending
investigation. POOOL’s compliance team receives ongoing training on AML/CFT
trends and typologies, and we will periodically review our KYC/AML systems
(potentially via independent audit) to ensure effectiveness. By maintaining strong
KYC/AML controls, POOOL not only complies with regulations but also protects its
own reputation and that of the nascent industry from being tarnished by financial
crime.

● Consumer Protection Measures: Protecting investors is of paramount importance
in POOOL’s model, given that we deal with retail investors who may be new to this
type of investment. To align with OJK’s consumer protection principles, we implement

several measures:

- Transparent Disclosures: Each investment offering is accompanied by an Offering
Document and a Key Facts Statement (KFS) that clearly lay out the nature of the
investment, rights of investors, all fees and costs, the risks involved (including
worst-case scenarios), and any limitations on liquidity . We avoid technical jargon or,
where it is necessary (like describing the legal structure or blockchain aspects), we
provide plain-language explanations so that investors can fully understand what they
are participating in. The KFS distills the essential information (asset details, expected
returns, lock-in period, fees, etc.) on a few pages for easy review, akin to a
prospectus summary.

- Cooling-off Period: As mentioned, we provide a minimum 2×24 hour (48-hour)
cooling-off period after an initial subscription during which an investor can cancel
for any reason and get a refund . This is prominently disclosed and is automated on
our platform (with a countdown timer shown for each subscription). This policy aligns
with best practices to ensure investors do not feel trapped in an investment if they
quickly change their mind or realize they made an error.

- Investment Limits: Although not yet formally regulated for our specific product, we
voluntarily adopt guidelines similar to equity crowdfunding rules regarding
investment limits based on income or net worth . For example, an investor may
be limited to investing no more than a certain percentage of their annual income
across POOOL offerings in a year, unless they are verified as an
accredited/high-net-worth investor. These limits protect individuals from
over-exposure to high-risk investments and will be adjusted to meet any specific
limits OJK sets during the sandbox. The platform’s onboarding process includes
investor education about not investing money they cannot afford to lose.

- Complaint Resolution: POOOL maintains a formal customer support and dispute
resolution process. Users can file complaints or raise issues via multiple channels
(in-app support ticket, email, or phone hotline). We have service level targets to
respond to and resolve complaints promptly (e.g., initial response within 1 business
day). If a complaint relates to a financial loss or potential misconduct, it is escalated
to our Compliance department for investigation. We document all complaints and
their resolution. In case of disputes, our terms provide for mediation or referring the
matter to OJK’s Alternatif Penyelesaian Sengketa (APS) for financial services, if
applicable, to ensure users have recourse to an impartial dispute resolution
mechanism. By handling complaints seriously and fairly, we aim to address issues
before they escalate, thus maintaining trust.

- Education and Literacy: We contribute to financial literacy by providing educational
resources about fractional investing, real estate risks, and blockchain. Through
articles, tutorials, webinars, and in-app warnings, we make sure investors are aware
of the risks (e.g., “property investments can fluctuate, there’s no guaranteed profit,
secondary market liquidity is uncertain”, etc.). This aligns with OJK’s mandate to
improve consumer understanding of financial products and helps users make

informed decisions .

● Data Protection and IT Security Compliance: POOOL complies with Indonesia’s
Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) and relevant OJK regulations regarding
cybersecurity and data protection. We have implemented an Information Security
Management System (ISMS) and are in the process of obtaining ISO 27001
certification to demonstrate adherence to international standards . All personal data
collected is used and stored in accordance with consent and purpose limitations, and
we have strong measures to protect data privacy. For instance, sensitive personal
data and documents are stored encrypted at rest, and all data in transit is protected
by TLS encryption . Access to customer data internally is strictly controlled under the
principle of least privilege; only staff with a need (e.g., compliance officers, customer
support handling an issue) can access certain data, and all access is logged and
monitored. We also comply with Kominfo (Ministry of Communication and IT)
regulations as a registered Electronic System Provider, ensuring that our systems are
hosted appropriately (with local data center requirements observed) and audited for
compliance. The platform undergoes regular vulnerability assessments and
penetration testing by independent experts at least annually . We are aligning
with OJK’s forthcoming IT risk management guidelines which likely mirror the existing
OJK regulations for digital banking and payment systems (e.g., requiring disaster
recovery plans, incident reporting within 24 hours of material incidents, etc.). In the
event of a data breach or cyber incident, we have an incident response plan that
includes notifying affected users and authorities (including OJK) in a timely manner
and taking steps to contain and remediate the issue. By taking data security
seriously, POOOL not only protects its users but also builds the kind of trust and
reliability that regulators expect from fintech operators .

● Organizational Structure and Governance: POOOL has established an
organization structure that supports compliance and sound risk management. We
have distinct functional teams for Technology, Operations, Finance & Compliance,
and Marketing/Business Development . Each department is led by experienced
professionals, and notably, we have appointed a Chief Compliance Officer who
ensures that all activities meet regulatory requirements and who serves as the
primary liaison with OJK. We also formed a Risk & Compliance Committee (RCC)
at the board or senior management level, which meets regularly to review
compliance reports, risk indicators, and any incidents . This committee oversees
critical areas such as the status of escrow funds and flows, customer protection
measures (like whether disclosures and cooling-off implementations are effective),
AML reporting, and adherence to sandbox limits/KPIs . Minutes of RCC meetings
and key risk dashboards are documented for transparency and can be provided to
regulators during audits or sandbox evaluations. All key personnel in management
have been vetted for fitness and propriety – for example, background checks for any
regulatory sanctions or criminal records, verification of relevant experience – aligning
with OJK’s fit and proper test expectations for fintech management . As a young
company, our team is lean, but we have access to external advisory from legal and
financial experts as needed. We maintain proper documentation of all policies,
Standard Operating Procedures, and an internal control framework, and these are
updated periodically to reflect any changes in regulation or business processes. Our

governance approach is one of continuous compliance – treating regulatory
adherence not just as a one-time hurdle but as an ongoing aspect of operations. We
believe this approach will serve us well as we graduate from the sandbox to fully
licensed status, since we are effectively behaving like a regulated entity from day
one.
In conclusion, POOOL’s compliance alignment efforts are comprehensive and ingrained in
the business. We recognize that trust from users and approval from regulators must be
earned through diligent adherence to rules and proactive risk management. By aligning our
operations with OJK’s regulations, guidelines, and supervisory expectations – in areas
ranging from fund handling, KYC/AML, consumer protection, to IT security and governance –
we aim to not only meet the minimum requirements but exceed them, showcasing POOOL
as a model for responsible innovation. Our participation in the regulatory sandbox is being
leveraged to fine-tune this alignment, and we are confident that by the time we seek a full
license, our compliance foundation will be solidly in place. This will ultimately benefit all
stakeholders: investors will have confidence in the platform’s integrity, regulators will see
their objectives fulfilled (financial inclusion with safety), and the company will be
well-positioned for sustainable, legally-compliant growth.

Risk Mitigation and Management
POOOL employs a comprehensive risk management strategy that aligns with OJK's
framework for fintech operators. We systematically identify, monitor, and mitigate risks
across multiple domains to protect our users and maintain operational stability.
## 1. Financial Risk
We mitigate financial risk through a segregated funds model where investor capital is held
in escrow, completely separate from POOOL's operational accounts. This ensures that even
in the event of POOOL's financial distress, investor funds remain secure. We maintain
capital reserves and liquidity buffers to absorb unexpected losses, and our internal controls
require dual approvals for all fund movements to prevent mismanagement or fraud.
## 2. Operational Risk
We mitigate operational risk by maintaining clear Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
and employing automation to reduce human error. Our technology infrastructure is built with
significant redundancy, including multiple servers, backup databases, and a secondary data

- Liquidity Risk and Secondary Market Governance
We acknowledge that fractional real estate investments carry liquidity risk. To mitigate this,
our secondary marketplace is designed as a controlled, OTC-style bulletin board for

activity is transparently reported to the OJK during the sandbox phase to ensure orderly
trading and investor protection.
- Cybersecurity and IT Risk
As an online platform, cybersecurity is a top priority. We employ a layered defense strategy,
including encryption for all sensitive data and regular vulnerability assessments and
penetration testing by independent experts. Our smart contracts are externally audited to
prevent vulnerabilities. We maintain a detailed Incident Response Plan to ensure rapid
response to any cyber threats, aligning with OJK’s expectations for robust IT risk
management.
- Legal and Regulatory Risk
We proactively manage legal and regulatory risks by engaging with regulators and legal
advisors. Our participation in the OJK regulatory sandbox provides a structured environment
for testing our model under regulatory oversight, reducing the risk of non-compliance. Our
legal documents and contracts are meticulously drafted to be transparent and fair to users.
## 6. Reputational Risk
Trust is fundamental to our business. We mitigate reputational risk through a commitment to
transparency, accountability, and customer-centric service. We provide clear, honest
communications, promptly address user complaints, and avoid making misleading claims.

Our proactive approach to compliance and open engagement with regulators helps build and
maintain trust in our platform.
